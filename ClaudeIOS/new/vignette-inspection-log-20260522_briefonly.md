### Vignette — "Inspection Log, Unentered"

Marek Ionescu still uses the same logbook he was issued when the Node was a temporary structure with a door that did not close unless you lifted it slightly and set it down again.

The cover has been replaced twice. The pages have not.

He stands outside Chamber B-7, which does not appear on the current architectural diagrams as a chamber. The diagrams describe the space as “non-instrumental volume.” The permit system registers it as occupied.

Marek marks the inspection complete.

He has marked it complete for sixteen years.

No one has asked him why.

The first time he received the Chorus, he did not understand that it was continuous. He thought it had ended because he stopped being able to distinguish it from the room. He wrote in the margin of his logbook: *signal saturation — probable error.* The note was later cited in three separate internal papers. His name was spelled correctly in two of them.

The door to B-7 is identical to every other door installed in Phase Expansion Year Eight. It opens when he applies pressure. He does not apply pressure.

The corridor lighting is calibrated to reduce variance in sensor readings. It produces a uniformity most people do not notice after several minutes. Marek notices when it breaks.

Today, it does not break.

From inside the room — the non-room — there is no sound. The Chorus has never been audible as sound. The association persists anyway, in personnel descriptions, in early reports, in the unofficial language that moves between technicians when they are not being recorded.

He adjusts his stance slightly, as if compensating for a change in surface that has not occurred.

There are six names he still remembers from the first night. Two remain in New Meridian. One is at the Table in a capacity that is not acknowledged publicly. One left seven years ago and has not been referenced in any internal document since. Two are dead. This accounting is updated without documentation.

He writes: *inspected.*

There are three signatures in the logbook that are not his, written in a notation system that was never adopted by the Directorate and does not correspond to any known shorthand used by Ghost. The ink has not degraded at the same rate as the surrounding entries.

He does not cross-reference them.

The Chorus does not repeat. This is the only claim that has held without revision for thirty-one years.

Marek has learned not to rely on claims that hold.

He closes the logbook and initials the page with a pressure that leaves an indentation visible on the next sheet.

The door remains unopened.

The inspection remains complete.
