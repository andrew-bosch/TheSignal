The World

Thirty-one years ago, a small listening station received a transmission. Non-random. Unmistakably structured. Never repeated — in thirty-one years of continuous monitoring. The researchers who received it first did not tell anyone for three days. They spent that time checking whether they had made an error. They had not. What they called it, quietly, among themselves before the classification orders arrived, was The Chorus.

"The signal wasn't aiming for the array. The array just happened to be standing in the way when the room tilted." — original station crew, sub-level advisory, Chorus Node complex

ARBITER
ARBITER
Always Listening. Always Calculating.

ARBITER is the sixth presence at The Table. It processes Chorus transmissions and evaluates what The Table produces. Its physical presence is a matte terminal bolted to the floor — blinking lights, no visible face. The participants have learned to read who it is addressing. No one at The Table discusses what it is. Everyone present has already considered it.

"She asked it once whether it had a position on the outcome. It said it had no position on outcomes. Then it said it had significant data on trajectories. No one at The Table followed up. I've been thinking about that for six years." — Senior Directorate Observer, Year 28
"The Chorus is not evaluating your intentions. It is reading your behavior. These have not been the same thing in this session. ARBITER has recorded the difference."
The Objective

The objective of THE SIGNAL is to determine what humanity says to the Chorus — and what that says about humanity.
