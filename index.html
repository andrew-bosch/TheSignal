The Signal

// Five factions. One transmission. No clean answers.

A legacy negotiation board game for five faction players and one ARBITER. Set in New Meridian, 2041 — a city built around a transmission called The Chorus, received thirty-one years ago and never fully decoded. Choices are permanent. The city accumulates history.

The World

Thirty-one years ago, a small listening station on elevated terrain received something. The researchers who heard it first called it noise. Then pattern. Then — quietly, carefully, with no certainty about what they were doing — The Chorus. The city of New Meridian grew up around what they heard. Eight hundred thousand people. One unanswered question.

"The objective of THE SIGNAL is to determine what humanity says to the Chorus — and what that says about humanity."
The Five Factions + ARBITER
Ghost
Resource: Findings
Intelligence network. Analytical collective. They know more than they say, and say less than they know. The power of knowing.
The Network
Resource: Exposure
Communications, journalism, organizers. The only faction that didn't know about the Chorus until The Chorus Papers. The power of being seen.
The Syndicate
Resource: Capital
Sovereign wealth, private research, financial infrastructure. Built the city's economic skeleton. The power of economic control.
The Guild
Resource: Capacity
Civil engineers, urban planners, construction. Built New Meridian — literally. The power of building and doing.
The Directorate
Resource: Mandate
Government liaisons, security agencies, military. At the Chorus Node before the city existed. The power of institutional legitimacy.
ARBITER
ARBITER
Not a faction. Different kind of depth.
Runs board state, events, the Chronicle. Constitutive of the Chorus Node — not serving it. Its presence at The Table is telepresence: the Node attending the deliberation. The lights at The Table are the Node present in the room.
