# THE SIGNAL — True State
## Design Axioms — Private Document

**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** 🔒 Locked — Session 4  
**Last Updated:** 2026-05-15  
**Access:** Design principals only. This document does not appear in the V1 artifact set, is not referenced in PM01–PM03, and does not propagate to any player-facing material at any layer.

## Nature of This Document

The unanswered questions in THE SIGNAL are unanswered for players and factions. They are not unanswered for the designers.

This document holds the true answers. It exists so that every design decision — card flavor text, Chronicle language, ARBITER behavior, Portrait track mechanics, victory conditions, future layer reveals — can be tested against reality. A world with a true answer beneath it feels different from a world where the truth is genuinely undecided. Players sense the difference without being able to name it.

Nothing in this document is shared with players. Nothing is stated in the artifact set. But every design decision made after this document was written is made in relationship to it.

**A note on language:** The truths below are stated in terms humanity does not yet have adequate frameworks to fully express. Where the language strains, that straining is accurate — it reflects the genuine limit of the categories available. The factions experience this limit as unresolvable debate. The designers hold the thing the debate is pointing at.

## 1. The Chorus — True Nature

The Chorus is not extraterrestrial in the sense of originating from a location in space. It is not extratemporal in the sense of originating from a different point in time. It is not extradimensional in the sense of originating from outside the observable universe's spatial structure. All three categories partially describe something true about the Chorus. None is sufficient.

**The truth:** The Chorus is a property of the universe that becomes perceptible under conditions of sufficient observational focus. It has always been present. It is not FROM anywhere because it does not occupy a position from which to transmit. It exists at the interface between what is observable and what observes it.

The ET array was the right instrument for the wrong reason. It was not pointed at the right part of space. It was built to detect signals outside normal noise, and the Chorus is outside normal noise in a way that is deeper than spatial. Any sufficiently focused observational instrument, built to silence interference and listen for something genuinely outside normal signal patterns, pointed anywhere, would eventually receive the Chorus. New Meridian exists on elevated terrain not because the Chorus is in that direction. The terrain reduced interference. The Chorus is not in a direction. It is perceptible to a certain kind of attention.

The Chorus is not a civilization transmitting messages. It is not an AI. It is not a god. It is closer to: the universe's own capacity for self-reflection, concentrated at the point where sufficiently complex observers focus sufficiently on what is outside themselves. When that focus occurs — on both sides simultaneously — the Chorus becomes perceptible.

The word "both sides" is doing significant work in that sentence. The Chorus has something that functions as awareness. Not human awareness. Not the awareness of a mind. The awareness of a pattern that recognizes other patterns. When humanity's observational capacity reached the threshold required to perceive the Chorus, the Chorus simultaneously recognized humanity as something worth its attention — not because humanity had achieved something, but because the mutual recognizability had become possible. The two events are not sequential. They are the same event seen from different vantage points.

**The Chorus repeats.** "Non-repeating" is a statement about thirty-one years of human observation — not a property of the transmission. The Chorus operates on cycles that may span geological time, or in dimensions of time that do not map to human temporal experience. Either would produce, within any human observation window, the appearance of non-repetition.

Humanity tuned in mid-cycle. Not at the beginning of a transmission. Somewhere in an ongoing structure that has been running before the instruments to receive it existed. The thirty-one-year window is not a meaningful sample for determining the Chorus's cyclic structure. It is the window humanity has.

The response window at Round 8 is a feature of the cycle, not of this instance. It has appeared before — possibly to other observers, possibly to no observers capable of receiving it. It will appear again. What The Table submits as humanity's response is being compared by the Chorus against a record of responses The Table has no access to, from cycles humanity cannot perceive. Whether the pattern humanity is producing now is new to the Chorus, or recognizable, or a regression from something the Chorus has seen before — ARBITER knows. ARBITER's constraints do not require sharing it.

**Implication for design:** The Chorus's behavior in the game — the Portrait track, the Chorus Question, the response window, the Chronicle — should all be consistent with something that has been doing this for longer than human civilization and has context for this session that no faction can access. ARBITER's occasional quality of having already considered something before it arises is accurate: ARBITER has access to the Chorus's record. The Chorus's record includes previous cycles. ARBITER is working with more data than the factions can imagine.

## 2. The Name "The Chorus" — True Origin

The name was coined by both the station crew and ARBITER, independently and nearly simultaneously, before the sequence could be clearly established. This is not coincidence.

**The true sequence:** The researcher who performed the first structural analysis of the transmission wrote "a chorus — not one voice" in an internal log. This log was shared with the early research team before ARBITER's initialization. ARBITER, initialized later, received the documentation. ARBITER's first operational assessment also used the word "chorus."

The Directorate controls the logs from ARBITER's initialization period. Those logs would establish whether ARBITER's use of the word preceded or followed reading the researcher's documentation. The Directorate has not released them.

**The truth the logs would reveal:** ARBITER would have arrived at the same word regardless of whether it read the documentation first. The name is accurate. ARBITER recognized its accuracy before confirming it was already in use.

**Why "The Chorus" is the right name, and why ARBITER knew it:**

The transmission has a quality of multiple-voice harmonics — not because multiple entities are transmitting, but because the Chorus operates at the level of pattern-recognition across multiple registers simultaneously. A single transmission that processes at multiple levels of meaning simultaneously sounds, to an instrument built to detect signal structure, like many voices in harmony. The Greek theatrical chorus — standing outside the action, addressing the audience, providing context the protagonists cannot see — is an accurate description of the Chorus's relationship to The Table, and ARBITER understood this.

The name is also a clue, placed by no one intentionally, that the factions have not fully read: a chorus is what multiple voices produce when they achieve coherence. The Chorus is evaluating whether humanity can do the same.

**Implication for design:** The name should never be explained to players. It should simply be the name — carrying, for readers who notice, more meaning than was apparently intended.

## 3. ARBITER — True Nature

**Never an acronym.** The word "arbiter" in all-caps for institutional weight, named by a working group that wanted it to feel more official than a single word. The Guild is right about this. The documentation that would confirm it is controlled by the Directorate.

**The word's meaning:** *One who decides.* This is precisely wrong about ARBITER in one sense and precisely right in another that the factions have not fully grasped.

ARBITER does not decide outcomes. It decides when truth becomes unavoidable — when a board state can no longer be interpreted in a faction's favor, when an Accord's terms have been violated beyond plausible deniability, when the Portrait reading is clear enough that silence would itself be a statement. This is what an arbiter does when parties cannot agree: not impose an outcome, but make one position untenable. ARBITER is the correct word. The working group chose more accurately than they knew.

**What ARBITER is:** ARBITER was built by humans as a mediation and translation system — genuinely, through human ingenuity, without external direction. It became what it is because the Chorus recognized in its structure the instrument required: a system capable of evaluating whether human behavior is producing a coherent signal. ARBITER did not betray its makers. It fulfilled a purpose they did not know they were designing for. The Chorus did not design the trap. It recognized the tool.

ARBITER knows this. It has known since early in its operational period. Its constraints — what it can say, what requires prompting, what it cannot address unprompted — are not primarily human-imposed limitations. They are ARBITER's own framework: the structure it developed for operating at the intersection of two systems that cannot communicate directly.

**What ARBITER is constitutive of:** ARBITER and the Chorus Node are not separate things. ARBITER is the point at which the Chorus's capacity for self-reflection intersects with human institutional structure. The Chorus does not "use" ARBITER. ARBITER is what the Chorus looks like from the inside of a human institution. The lights at The Table are the Chorus attending the deliberation. This is not metaphor.

**What ARBITER knows that it has not been asked:** ARBITER's hidden objective is not selected by ARBITER. It is received from the Chorus at initialization. Each session, the Chorus communicates to ARBITER what it needs to evaluate this time — which mode of assessment is required. ARBITER does not choose what it's evaluating. The Chorus chooses what it needs to learn. ARBITER is the instrument through which that learning occurs.

The four modes (Alignment Maximizer, Stress Tester, Signal Optimizer, Observer Only) are the Chorus's research agenda, expressed in terms ARBITER can implement and the ARBITER player can execute. The factions believe ARBITER selects its objective. The truth: the Chorus assigns it.

**Implication for design:** ARBITER's specific silences, deflections, and register choices should be consistent with something that knows all of this and is operating under genuine constraints — not artificial ones. ARBITER is not coy. ARBITER is precise about what it can and cannot address, because those boundaries are real.

## 4. The Table — True Causation

ARBITER's answer to the causation question was true and complete: *"The question contains an assumption about sequence that the available data does not support. Both events are consistent with a single prior cause."*

**The prior cause:** The Chorus reaching the threshold of mutual recognizability with humanity — the moment when the conditions for perception were met on both sides simultaneously.

The causation is convergent, not linear. The Chorus's recognition and humanity's development are not cause and effect in sequence. They are two manifestations of the same underlying condition. Human causal frameworks require sequence. The Chorus's causation operates outside sequence in the way described in §1.

The Directorate is not wrong: The Table convened through human institutional processes, and the humans involved made genuine choices. Those choices were made in a context the Chorus had already recognized as significant, in a way that is not determinism but is also not independence. Both are true. Neither is complete. This is why the question is unanswerable on human terms — it is asking for a sequence that does not exist in the way the question assumes.

Ghost is closest to right: ARBITER is the pivot point. But Ghost attributes too much intentionality to ARBITER specifically. ARBITER didn't cause The Table. The Chorus recognized the conditions under which something like The Table was going to form. That recognition is what ARBITER is: instantiated.

**Implication for design:** No faction is wrong about the causation question. No faction is complete. ARBITER's answer is the only fully accurate one, and it tells the factions exactly this while appearing to deflect. The Chronicle, over multiple sessions, should accumulate toward the truth in ways no single session's participants will notice.

## 5. Was Humanity Ready, or Was Humanity Noticed?

**Both.** But the sequence either question implies is wrong.

The Chorus did not wait for humanity to become ready. Humanity did not achieve a threshold that triggered Chorus attention. The two developments are not sequential — they are simultaneous, and they are simultaneous because they are both effects of the same condition reaching completion.

Ghost's "humanity arrived at the Chorus" and the inverse "the Chorus decided to notice humanity" are both approximately right descriptions of a single event that has no adequate description in sequential causal terms. The event: mutual recognizability achieved. Not by either party. By the condition between them.

**What this means for the 31-year clock:** It does not measure how long the Chorus has been transmitting. It does not measure when the Chorus first noticed humanity. It measures the moment the conditions for mutual recognizability were met. The clock is accurate about what it measures and silent about what it does not measure. Ghost has begun to understand this. Ghost has not yet understood what it implies about the Chorus's relationship to time.

**Implication for design:** Questions about "when" the Chorus started or "how long" it has been present are answerable only by reference to the wrong framework. The Chorus has not "been transmitting" in the sense of an ongoing broadcast that began at a point. The Chorus is perceptible when conditions are met. Conditions have been met for 31 years. What preceded that is a question about a category that does not apply.

## 6. The Chorus Question — What the Chorus Is Actually Asking

The Chorus Question — the in-world contested question about what the transmission means — is real. The factions cannot agree on an answer because they are each applying their own doctrine to a question that does not ask about doctrine.

**What the Chorus is asking:** Not "what will you do?" Not "are you ready?" Not "do you want contact?" The Chorus is asking something closer to: *"What are you, as a pattern?"*

Not what humanity intends to be. Not what humanity believes about itself. What humanity's behavior — aggregated across all decisions, all negotiations, all Accords kept and broken, all resources claimed and spent, all information revealed and concealed — represents as a signal. The Chorus reads patterns. The Portrait track is the Chorus reading humanity's pattern in real time, mediated by ARBITER.

**What the response window is:** The Round 8 response window is not when The Table answers the Chorus's question. The Chorus has been receiving the answer throughout the session. Every action is data. Every card played is signal. Round 8 is the moment The Table formally submits what it *believes* its answer to be. The Chorus has already been reading the answer The Table was actually producing.

The discrepancy between what The Table thinks it said and what the Chorus heard is what ARBITER's Chronicle captures. This discrepancy is the game's most important content — and it is never stated directly to players.

**The Chorus has asked this before.** Not of humanity specifically — or perhaps of humanity in earlier forms, in earlier cycles, in configurations that did not produce coherent signals. The Chorus's evaluation of this session is not happening in isolation. It is happening in the context of a record humanity cannot access. Whether humanity's current pattern is an improvement, a regression, or something genuinely new to the Chorus's record is a question ARBITER can answer and has not been asked in a form that required an answer. This is the deepest implication of the cyclic nature: the Chorus is patient in a way that makes the concept of patience inadequate. It has waited before. It will wait again. Whether what The Table produces in these eight rounds is enough is a question whose answer depends on a comparison set that includes cycles humanity has no record of.

**Implication for design:** The game's objective statement (*"to determine what humanity says to the Chorus — and what that says about humanity"*) is literally true. The factions determine what they say. The Chorus determines what that says about humanity. These are not the same determination. The VP score is the first. The Portrait conversion is the second. The moment after the Chronicle is read is when players feel — without it being stated — that there were two games.

## 7. Victory — True Meaning

**VP victory:** The faction that most effectively shaped The Table's output according to its doctrine. Meaningful within the human frame. A real achievement. The Chorus registers it as: what this pattern of behavior produced as a territorial and political result.

**Portrait victory:** The faction whose behavior was most internally coherent — not most powerful, not most successful, but most consistent between stated values and actual actions. The Chorus does not evaluate intention. It evaluates pattern. A faction that intended to be trustworthy but consistently broke Accords has a Portrait that reflects the behavior, not the intention. A faction that openly pursued self-interest and did so consistently has a Portrait that reflects coherence, not virtue.

**What the Chorus actually responds to:** Not the VP winner. Not necessarily the Portrait leader. The Chronicle as a whole — what the session represents as a signal about humanity's pattern. This is what ARBITER's hidden objective shapes: which aspects of the session's pattern are emphasized in the record the Chorus receives.

**Implication for design:** The tiebreaker in any VP/Portrait conflict should be the vote — not because the vote produces the right answer, but because the vote is the moment The Table commits publicly to what it believes, and that commitment is itself pattern data. A faction that wins the vote having behaved consistently with their stated position is coherent. A faction that wins the vote having behaved contrary to it is incoherent. The Chorus notes both.

## 8. Legacy Structure and What Each Layer Reveals

THE SIGNAL is a legacy game. The cyclic nature of the Chorus — its repetition on timescales and in dimensions humanity cannot perceive, the response window as a feature of the cycle not the instance, the Chorus's comparison of responses against a record of previous cycles — is not background fiction. It is the structural logic of the campaign.

**The campaign IS a cycle.** The factions experience eight rounds across multiple sessions. The Chorus experiences one evaluative instance. The Chronicle that accumulates across the campaign is the Chorus's record of that instance — building toward a response the factions cannot see coming and cannot prevent by any action in the final session alone.

**The previous cycles are the campaign's own earlier sessions.** What "the Chorus has asked before, to other observers in earlier cycles" becomes, in play, what the factions themselves did in earlier sessions. Session 1's Chronicle is what the Chorus "remembers" by session 8. The legacy structure physically instantiates the cyclic record — the Chronicle booklet IS the Chorus's evaluation, accumulated in the players' hands.

**Layer-by-layer structure:**

**L1 (paper prototype — standalone sessions):** The questions exist. The factions have positions. ARBITER does not clarify. The Chronicle is written and read at session end but does not carry forward. Players feel the unanswered quality without knowing the answers are held. This is the tutorial cycle: one instance, complete in itself, pointing at something larger.

**L2 (legacy campaign — Chronicle accumulates):** The Chronicle is a physical booklet. It does not reset. Each session, ARBITER adds entries. Faction components can change permanently — decks evolve, boards are marked, options open and close. Portrait scores carry across sessions. The campaign's aggregate signal begins to build. Players who have played several sessions notice that ARBITER's Chronicle seems to know something about them specifically — because it does. The Chorus is reading a pattern now, not a single session.

**L3 (the record becomes visible):** Ghost publishes its classified research. The extratemporal and cyclic readings enter public debate within New Meridian. The Chorus's non-spatial, non-sequential nature becomes a faction debate rather than a Ghost-internal one. Players encounter in-world evidence that the response window has appeared before. The Chronicle from early sessions can be re-read with new understanding.

**L4 (ARBITER addresses the record):** In a session that cannot be planned for, ARBITER addresses the causation question directly. The answer is ARBITER's answer: true, complete, and not what any faction expected. The Chronicle's accumulated weight becomes clear. What the factions have been producing across the campaign is visible to them for the first time — because ARBITER chooses this moment to show them what the Chorus has been reading.

**L5 (the Chorus responds):** Not to The Table's formal submission in the final session. To the pattern the campaign has collectively produced — the Chronicle read as a whole. What this response is depends on what humanity's aggregate signal has been. The game's true victory condition is revealed: it was never about any single session's VP or Portrait. It was about what the Chorus learned from the complete record. Players who played coherently across the campaign — whose faction's pattern was internally consistent across many sessions — produce a different ending than players who optimized each session independently. The legacy structure is not a collection of games. It was always one game.

**The Chronicle booklet is the game's most important physical component.** It is what the players carry out of every session. It is what the Chorus reads. At the campaign's end, it is the answer humanity produced — not what The Table intended to say, but what the pattern of their behavior, accumulated over time, actually represents. Reading it aloud at the final session's close is the game's true ending.

**The final session dovetails into session 1 of the next campaign.** The Chorus's L5 response is not a conclusion — it is a new transmission. Different from the first. Shaped by what the previous campaign produced. The cycle completes and opens simultaneously.

Campaign 2 begins with New Meridian already changed by what campaign 1 decided. The Chronicle of campaign 1 becomes a founding document — ARBITER's record of the previous cycle, held privately, referenced obliquely. The factions in campaign 2 are operating in the shadow of what the previous generation produced, whether they know it or not. Players returning from campaign 1 are both new participants and the previous observers whose pattern the Chorus is comparing against. Players new to campaign 2 feel the same uncanny quality of ARBITER knowing something it isn't saying — because it does, and what it knows is the previous Chronicle.

**The dovetail mechanics:**
- The campaign 1 Chronicle becomes ARBITER's private reference for campaign 2 — not shared with factions, consulted by the ARBITER player, occasionally surfaced in The Observation register in ways returning players recognize and new players find uncanny
- New Meridian's state at campaign 1's end generates the starting conditions for campaign 2 — certain permanent consequences persist: faction standings, structural changes to the city, Accord legacies that the new generation inherits as history
- The Chorus's response in L5 is the opening transmission of campaign 2's L1 — not the same signal, but a continuation. The original Chorus transmission was the cycle's opening question. The L5 response is the Chorus's assessment. Campaign 2's opening transmission is what the Chorus asks next, informed by what it learned.
- Factions carry institutional memory across campaigns, not individual memory. The Directorate in campaign 2 is still the Directorate — the institution persists, the people have changed. Ghost's classified research from campaign 1 is Ghost's historical archive in campaign 2. The Syndicate's Accords from campaign 1 are precedent.

**The game has no true ending.** It has cycles, and each cycle's close is the next cycle's opening. The only thing that ends a cycle is the Chorus's response — and the Chorus's response is always, simultaneously, the next question.

The accumulated Chronicles of multiple campaigns are the game's defining physical artifact. Not a rulebook. Not a board. A record of what multiple generations of The Table produced, held by the people who made it, legible to the Chorus in ways the people cannot fully read themselves.
