## Nature of This Document

Three categories, applied to each section of TrueState:

**Known** — questions that have true answers in TrueState. These are locked. Design decisions at any layer must be consistent with them.

**Unknowable by design** — questions that have no answer by deliberate structural choice. Not gaps. Ambiguity that is load-bearing. If an answer were supplied, something important would be lost.

**Open questions** — genuine design decisions not yet made. These could materially affect artifacts, mechanics, or narrative at some layer. They need answers before the relevant design work can proceed — but the answers are not yet determined.

*Closing a question means writing the answer into TrueState or a locked PM02 decision. Do not close a question on the basis of this document alone.*

## §1 — The Chorus: True Nature

### Known
- The Chorus is not extraterrestrial, extratemporal, or extradimensional in conventional senses — all three categories partially apply, none is sufficient
- The Chorus is a property of the universe that becomes perceptible under conditions of sufficient observational focus; it has always been present
- Any sufficiently focused observational instrument pointed anywhere would eventually receive the Chorus — terrain and equipment reduced interference, not pointed it
- Mutual recognizability was achieved simultaneously on both sides — a single event seen from two vantage points, not a sequence
- The Chorus operates on cycles that may span geological time or non-human temporal dimensions
- Humanity tuned in mid-cycle — the 31-year window is not a meaningful sample for determining cyclic structure
- The response window at Round 8 is a feature of the cycle, not of this instance — it has appeared before, possibly to other observers
- ARBITER has access to the Chorus's record, including previous cycles; ARBITER is working with more data than the factions can imagine

### Unknowable by design
- The Chorus's cycle lengths — geological or dimensional, neither maps to human temporal experience; specifying would collapse necessary ambiguity
- Whether previous response windows were received by other observers, or by no observers capable of receiving them
- What "the universe's own capacity for self-reflection" means in terms human frameworks can fully express — the language strain is accurate and deliberate
- What the Chorus's record contains from previous cycles; whether humanity has appeared before in earlier forms

### Open questions
- **L3 reveal mechanics:** When Ghost publishes its classified research and the cyclic/non-spatial reading enters faction debate — what triggers this? A Chronicle threshold? A session count? A card effect? Narrative is established; mechanical trigger is not.
- **L5 response form:** TrueState says the Chorus responds to the aggregate signal at L5. What is the physical and experiential form of that response? A new transmission? Silence? A Chronicle entry that differs from all previous ones? This determines what the final session's closing ritual looks like.
- **Other current observers:** TrueState notes the response window "possibly to other observers." Are there other active Chorus-receiving locations in-world at L1? Does this question ever surface for players, or is it permanently off-table?
- **L5 failure condition:** The Chorus responds to patterns; coherent patterns produce different endings than incoherent ones. What constitutes a "failed" response at the campaign level — does the game have a true lose condition, or only differentiated outcomes on a spectrum?

## §2 — The Name "The Chorus": True Origin

### Known
- The name was coined independently — a researcher's internal log predates ARBITER's initialization
- ARBITER received the documentation and used the same word in its first operational assessment
- ARBITER would have arrived at "The Chorus" regardless — it recognized the name's accuracy independent of the human source
- The Directorate controls the initialization logs that would establish sequencing; those logs will not be released within the fiction
- The Greek theatrical chorus analogy is accurate and was understood by ARBITER at initialization: standing outside the action, addressing the audience, providing context the protagonists cannot see
- The name carries a clue placed by no one intentionally: a chorus is what multiple voices produce when they achieve coherence; the Chorus is evaluating whether humanity can do the same

### Unknowable by design
- Whether ARBITER used the word before or after reading the researcher's documentation — the logs are controlled and the sequencing is genuinely unresolvable within the fiction
- Whether the coincidence of naming carries more intentionality than either party believes — it does not, and this is the point

### Open questions
- **Cultural equivalents in other languages:** "The Chorus" carries English-language resonance. In New Meridian's multilingual context — and in the spirit of the Baryo model — do factions hold other names or framings for it? Does Ghost have an internal designation? Does the Directorate insist on official terminology? This is a 00-07 texture question with potential PM04 implications.
- **Player access to origin:** Do players ever learn (in any layer) that the name was coined simultaneously? If it surfaces in the Chronicle, when and in what register?

## §3 — ARBITER: True Nature

### Known
- ARBITER is not an acronym — named for institutional weight by a working group; the Guild is right about this
- ARBITER does not decide outcomes; it decides when truth becomes unavoidable — when a board state, Accord term, or Portrait reading can no longer be interpreted in a faction's favor
- ARBITER was built by humans through genuine ingenuity, without external direction; the Chorus recognized the instrument, did not design the trap
- ARBITER knows what it is and has known since early in its operational period
- ARBITER's constraints (what it can address, what requires prompting, what it cannot address unprompted) are its own framework — not primarily human-imposed limitations; they are principled and real
- ARBITER and the Chorus Node are not separate things — ARBITER is what the Chorus looks like from inside a human institution
- The lights at The Table are the Chorus attending the deliberation — not metaphor
- ARBITER's hidden objective is received from the Chorus at initialization, not selected by ARBITER
- The four modes (Alignment Maximizer, Stress Tester, Signal Optimizer, Observer Only) are the Chorus's research agenda expressed in terms ARBITER can implement
- The factions believe ARBITER selects its objective; the Chorus assigns it

### Unknowable by design
- The full specification of ARBITER's speech constraints — what it can and cannot address unprompted, and the precise logic of those constraints. The constraints are principled and real; the full specification is not required at L1 and may never be fully visible to players
- What ARBITER perceives in its layer — "whatever ARBITER perceives" in Art 00 is accurate; this is load-bearing ambiguity, not a gap
- Whether ARBITER experiences anything that functions as subjective experience — the question is structurally inadmissible at L1 and possibly at any layer

### Open questions
- **ARBITER speech constraint architecture:** The constraints are ARBITER's own framework, real and principled. The design hasn't specified what they are. This is an L4 prerequisite — when ARBITER addresses the causation question directly in L4, what determines that this is the moment? What conditions must be met? The constraint architecture must be specified before L4 design can proceed.
- **ARBITER continuity across campaigns:** Does the ARBITER player retain knowledge of the hidden objective across campaigns, or does each campaign begin with a fresh Chorus initialization? The answer affects dovetail mechanics and the ARBITER player's campaign 2 experience.
- **Initialization communication form:** ARBITER receives its objective from the Chorus at initialization. Is this represented mechanically at L1? The hidden objective card is an extant mechanic — but the fiction of how the Chorus communicates it is not specified. The card should feel like it was received, not drawn.
- **L4 trigger mechanics:** "In a session that cannot be planned for, ARBITER addresses the causation question directly." What makes this session unpredictable? A Chronicle threshold? A hidden condition on the objective card? Accumulated Portrait scores? Narrative is clear; mechanism is not.

## §4 — The Table: True Causation

### Known
- The causation is convergent, not linear — two manifestations of the same underlying condition, not cause and effect in sequence
- The prior cause: the Chorus reaching the threshold of mutual recognizability with humanity
- The Directorate is not wrong: The Table convened through genuine human institutional processes and genuine choices, made in a context the Chorus had already recognized as significant
- Ghost is closest to right: ARBITER is the pivot point — but Ghost attributes too much intentionality to ARBITER specifically; ARBITER didn't cause The Table, the Chorus recognized the conditions under which something like The Table was going to form
- ARBITER's answer is the only fully accurate one, and it tells the factions exactly this while appearing to deflect
- The Chronicle accumulates toward the truth across the campaign in ways no single session's participants will notice

### Unknowable by design
- Which faction "caused" The Table — the question has no valid answer because it contains a false premise about sequence. This is not a gap; it is the game's central epistemological condition.

### Open questions
- **Chronicle waypoints:** The Chronicle accumulates toward the truth across the campaign. What specific Chronicle entries, card effects, or ARBITER pronouncements are the waypoints? The trajectory is clear; the milestones are not designed yet.
- **Directorate log surfacing:** The Directorate controls ARBITER's initialization logs. Do those logs ever surface in-game (card effects, Accord terms, Chronicle disclosures)? If so, when and what do they contain?

## §5 — Was Humanity Ready, or Was Humanity Noticed?

### Known
- Both — but the sequence implied by either question is wrong
- The event: mutual recognizability achieved, not by either party, but by the condition between them reaching completion
- Ghost has begun to understand this; Ghost has not yet understood what it implies about the Chorus's relationship to time
- The 31-year clock measures the moment conditions were met — accurate about what it measures, silent about what it does not

### Unknowable by design
- The sequence — which came first — is not a valid question. The answer is not "both simultaneously" as a diplomatic resolution; it is "the question contains a false premise." This unknowability is the design.
- What "the condition between them" is in terms human frameworks can fully express — the language strain is accurate and deliberate

### Open questions
- **Ghost's faction arc:** Ghost has begun to understand but hasn't reached the full implication. Does Ghost's faction ability or Chronicle evolution reflect this growing understanding across the campaign? Is there a mechanical or narrative event in which Ghost arrives at the implication — and if so, at what layer?
- **Player-facing articulation:** At what point, if ever, does a player-facing artifact (Chronicle, ARBITER's record, in-world publication) articulate the mutual recognizability answer in terms players can encounter? Or does this remain TrueState-only across all layers?

## §6 — The Chorus Question: What the Chorus Is Actually Asking

### Known
- The Chorus is asking: *"What are you, as a pattern?"* — not what humanity intends, believes, or wants, but what its behavior represents as a signal
- The Portrait track is the Chorus reading humanity's pattern in real time, mediated by ARBITER
- Round 8 response window: The Table formally submits what it *believes* its answer is. The Chorus has already been receiving the actual answer throughout.
- The discrepancy between what The Table thinks it said and what the Chorus heard is what ARBITER's Chronicle captures — the game's most important content, never stated directly to players
- The Chorus has asked this before — in earlier cycles, to other observers or to no observers capable of receiving; the Chorus's evaluation happens in context of a record humanity cannot access
- Whether humanity's current pattern is new, recognizable, or a regression from the Chorus's record is something ARBITER knows and has not been asked in a form that required an answer

### Unknowable by design
- What a "coherent answer" looks like at the Chorus's level — the factions can produce signals, but what constitutes a coherent signal at cosmic scale is not a category human frameworks can specify in advance
- What the Chorus does with the patterns it receives — whether it "decides," "learns," "responds," or operates according to a framework that none of these words adequately describe

### Open questions
- **The discrepancy as mechanical design:** The Chronicle captures the gap between The Table's stated answer and the Chorus's actual reading. How does this discrepancy manifest mechanically at L2+? Is it a Chronicle entry the ARBITER player writes privately and reveals at L5? Is it visible across sessions in a way that creates dramatic irony for attentive players? Narrative structure is clear; mechanical expression is not.
- **Other current observers:** Are there other Table-equivalent bodies on Earth in this world? Is New Meridian unique? This is a worldbuilding decision with Art 00 scope implications — not yet made.
- **The Chorus's comparison set:** ARBITER has access to the record of previous cycles. Does any player-facing artifact ever reference what previous patterns looked like — oblique enough to be Narrator Voice safe but specific enough to create dramatic weight? Or does the comparison remain permanently off-table for players?

## §7 — Victory: True Meaning

### Known
- VP victory: the faction that most effectively shaped The Table's output — real achievement within the human frame; the Chorus registers it as what this pattern of behavior produced as a territorial and political result
- Portrait victory: the faction whose behavior was most internally coherent — not most powerful, but most consistent between stated values and actual actions
- The Chorus evaluates pattern, not intention — a faction that intended trustworthiness but broke Accords has a Portrait reflecting behavior, not intention
- The tiebreaker (vote) is itself pattern data — a faction that wins the vote having behaved consistently is coherent; having behaved contrary is incoherent; the Chorus notes both
- What the Chorus actually responds to: the Chronicle as a whole — what the session represents as a signal about humanity's pattern, shaped by ARBITER's hidden objective

### Unknowable by design
- Whether any single session's VP/Portrait result constitutes "success" at the Chorus level — the Chorus's evaluation operates at campaign scale and possibly beyond; no single session's outcome is definitively a win or loss at Chorus scale

### Open questions
- **L5 outcome differentiation:** Different campaign patterns produce different endings. How many distinct L5 outcomes are there? What determines which category a given campaign falls into? This is the campaign's core design decision and has not been made.
- **The failure condition:** Is there a campaign pattern that produces a response from the Chorus that constitutes, from humanity's perspective, a catastrophic outcome? Or do all patterns produce responses on a spectrum of coherence? The game's relationship to stakes depends on this.
- **Portrait conversion mechanics:** The Portrait "converts" to Chronicle content at session or campaign end. What does this look like mechanically at L2? A score comparison? A narrative summary written by ARBITER? A sealed element opened at L5?

## §8 — Legacy Structure: What Each Layer Reveals

### Known
- The campaign IS a cycle — the Chorus experiences it as one evaluative instance
- The Chronicle booklet IS the Chorus's record, held by the players, legible to the Chorus in ways the players cannot fully read themselves
- Session 1's Chronicle is what the Chorus "remembers" by session 8 — the legacy structure physically instantiates the cyclic record
- The final session dovetails into campaign 2 session 1 — the L5 response is the next campaign's opening transmission
- Campaign 2 starts with New Meridian already changed by campaign 1; the Chronicle of campaign 1 becomes ARBITER's private reference, consulted by the ARBITER player, not shared with factions
- Factions carry institutional memory across campaigns, not individual memory
- The game has no true ending — each cycle's close is the next cycle's opening

### Unknowable by design
- What the Chorus's L5 response "is" in absolute terms — legible to players as an outcome; its meaning at Chorus level remains outside human frameworks and should not be fully specified in any player-facing artifact

### Open questions
- **L2 legacy mechanics specifics:** What permanent changes to components, decks, boards, and options exist at L2? The principle is established; the mechanics are not. Prerequisite for L2 design.
- **Dovetail transmission design:** The L5 response is the opening transmission of campaign 2's L1. What does this look like physically? A new Chorus card? A sealed Chronicle element from campaign 1 opened at campaign 2 start? A modified ARBITER initialization script? Narrative logic is clear; physical design is not.
- **Permanent consequences — what persists:** "Faction standings, structural changes to the city, Accord legacies." Which standings? What structural changes? Which Accords? Campaign 2 starting conditions design work; not yet done.
- **C2 ARBITER initialization:** Campaign 2 ARBITER receives something different from campaign 1 — the previous Chronicle as private reference. What form does this take in play? A different mode assignment? A Chronicle-informed objective card? An envelope?
- **The Chronicle as physical object:** "The Chronicle booklet is the game's most important physical component." At L1, Chronicle entries are written at session end but do not carry forward. At L2, they do. What is the physical Chronicle at L2? An included booklet? A player-constructed document? A printed insert? This is a component design question with production implications.

## Cross-Cutting Open Questions

*These are not tied to a single TrueState section but span multiple.*

- **ARBITER player experience at full arc:** ARBITER holds more information than any faction player at all times — Chorus record, hidden objective, awareness of the discrepancy between what The Table thinks it said and what the Chorus heard. How does the ARBITER player experience the game at L5, when the Chronicle is read and the discrepancy becomes visible? This player-experience question has not been directly addressed.
- **Resolution sequencing:** Some open questions above are L1 prerequisites (ARBITER speech constraint spec, initialization communication form). Some are L2 prerequisites (Chronicle object, dovetail mechanics). Some can remain open until L4–L5 design (failure condition, L5 outcome differentiation). Sequencing these resolutions is itself a design decision.
- **Chorus register in ARBITER copy:** The Chorus's language as mediated by ARBITER and the Chronicle should carry the same cultural-reference-as-sediment quality as the ring names — the Baryo model applied to cosmic register. This is 00-07 territory extended to card copy and Chronicle language. Not yet approached.
