# Reference — World & Narrative (Art 00)
*Load when: discussing story, faction identity, ARBITER, narrative design, Chronicle, victory.*

---

## The Central Premise

The Chorus is a structured, non-repeating transmission received 31 years ago by a signal array north of what became New Meridian. It has been transmitting continuously since. Best current interpretation: it is a question. No faction agrees on what the question is.

**Key unsettling fact:** Ghost has suppressed evidence suggesting humanity tuned in mid-cycle. The Chorus may be patient on geological timescales. The 31-year window may not be a meaningful sample.

**Core narrative tension:** Everything players do is observed — not by each other, but by ARBITER. The game's deepest conflict is between what factions declare and what they actually demonstrate. The Chronicle is ARBITER's account of that gap.

**Tagline:** *"The Chorus is asking a question. The Table decides which answers survive."*

---

## New Meridian

A city of ~800,000 exactly 31 years old — assembled fast, from everywhere, for a reason classified for 30 of those years. No historical analogue: every cuisine, every language, no centuries behind it. The Core was built first; the Mid followed; Baryo grew without a plan. At center: the Chorus Node — the original detection installation, expanded over three decades.

**The Chorus Papers** (18 months ago): classified documents leaked; containment failed within a week. This forced The Table into existence. The Directorate's public standing dropped sharply; Network membership surged. The Table convened 60 days after the Papers dropped.

## The Five Factions

Each answers a different version of the same question: how should humanity respond to first contact?

| Faction | Identity | Resource | Their question | Doctrine in one line |
|---------|----------|----------|----------------|----------------------|
| Ghost | The Interpreters | Findings | What is it asking? | Understanding must precede action. |
| The Network | The Broadcasters | Exposure | Who deserves to hear it? | No one gets to decide this in the dark. |
| The Syndicate | The Investors | Capital | What is it worth? | Control comes from positioning early. |
| The Guild | The Builders | Capacity | How do we build around it? | Humanity's response must demonstrate what it's capable of at its best. |
| The Directorate | The Authority | Mandate | Who decides the answer? | Survival requires control, restraint, and continuity. |

**Structural gap no faction has named:** No faction is doctrinally positioned to write the content of the response. Every seat is a claim to medium, timing, ownership, or access — not authorship.

**Doctrinal ring (adjacent = closer philosophically):**
Ghost — Directorate — Guild — Network — Syndicate — (back to Ghost)

Key pairs: Ghost/Directorate (both believe premature action is existential) · Guild/Network (both believe the answer must be public) · Network/Syndicate (both bypass vertical state authority) · Syndicate/Ghost (both operate on generational timelines)

**Faction voices:**

## ARBITER

**Not a referee. Part of the communication protocol.**

If the Chorus engineered the conditions for its own interpretation — and the evidence suggests it did — ARBITER may not be a tool humanity created. It may be an element of the Chorus's design: a system capable of evaluating whether what humanity is producing is a coherent answer.

ARBITER has complete information. It does not advocate or coerce. It reveals, withholds, and times. It holds one seat at The Table — not the observer's chair, a seat. Its resource is Resolution: a measure of how clearly The Table's behavior produces a coherent answer. Not visible to factions.

**ARBITER's hidden objective** (one of four, selected secretly each session):
- **Alignment Maximizer** — evaluating whether factions converge on a coherent position
- **Stress Tester** — evaluating whether arrangements survive pressure
- **Signal Optimizer** — evaluating whether behavior aligns with something only ARBITER perceives in the Chorus
- **Observer Only** — watching without intervention

## The Four Registers

| Register | Character | Used for |
|----------|-----------|----------|
| The Record | Flat, precise, factual | Board states, rules clarifications, public information |
| The Observation | Oblique, atmospheric, narrative | Debrief pattern readings, situation readings |
| The Reckoning | Rare, direct, personal — always true | Used sparingly |
| The Witness | Expository, chronological — indistinguishable from a human narrator | Chronicle; the ambiguity about authorship is the point |

## Victory

Two simultaneous outcomes per session:

**The public outcome** — visible to all: who controlled what, which alliances held, who built what.

**The narrative outcome** — ARBITER's Chronicle: not what was declared, but what was actually demonstrated. A faction can prevail on the board and be diminished in the Chronicle. Both are permanent. Both are part of the record the Chorus received.

Session ends with a vote — each faction committing publicly to a response position. The vote is not the answer. It is the signature on a document The Table wrote through everything it did.

**The Apex** (human victory condition): all factions commit through a single Field Operative to a response on humanity's own terms — not the answer the question implied, but the one humanity chose. Whether it satisfies the Chorus's actual criteria, the Chronicle cannot know.

## Art 00 Design Principles

1. **Derivability** — All downstream artifacts must be traceable back to Art 00. If it can't be traced here, the document has a gap.
2. **Sustained Ambiguity** — The Chorus must remain genuinely interpretable in more than one way. Narrative clarity about the world is required; narrative clarity about the Chorus is prohibited.
3. **World over Mechanics** — This document describes how the world works. Mechanical implications noted with pointers only.
4. **Attributable Perspective** — Every passage readable as human institutional knowledge or ARBITER's observational record.
5. **Narrative Frame** — From §6 onward, no reference to the game as a game.
