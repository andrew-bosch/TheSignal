## 1. Board Elements (The Overview)

**District Tiles (The Civic Grid)**

The visual layout of New Meridian projected by MIRROR isn't a natural map—it's a political compromise turned digital reality. Originally, Dr. Aris Thorne (Chief Data Architect) designed the grid to be a fluid, heat-map topography based on population density and socio-economic flow. But Colonel Jax Vane (Directorate Security Liaison) vetoed the design, demanding rigid, hard-coded infrastructural zones based on blast doors, riot barricades, and power-grid kill switches. Vane won. The resulting grid is brutalist and functional. It represents how the city can be partitioned, locked down, and violently controlled in an emergency.

**Influence Level Marker (Dominant)**

This is a direct, localized derivative of the Holt Index. While the Holt Index tracks raw physical presence (boots on the ground, operative capacity), Influence tracks administrative control. When a faction hits Dominance in a district, MIRROR officially recognizes their infrastructural override. They now control the traffic grids, municipal drone routing, and utility outputs for that zone. MIRROR monitors this relentlessly because ARBITER requires a constant calculation of who actually holds the keys to the city's logistical choke points and civic arteries in order to measure true geopolitical leverage.

**Contested Markers (Tension)**

This is monitored in real-time through New Meridian's "Nervous System"—a city-wide, hyper-surveillance mesh of biometric, acoustic, and thermal sensors originally installed for predictive medical emergencies. The system has been weaponized by MIRROR. It constantly listens for the acoustic signatures of gunfire, tracks sudden spikes in crowd heart rates via municipal wristlinks, and detects encrypted radio bursts. When the algorithm flags a localized threshold breach, MIRROR automatically throws up a pulsating thermal/kinetic static overlay on The Overview, signaling that violence or chaos has erupted.

## 2. Shared Supply & Documents

**The Reservoir**

This represents the raw, untapped potential of New Meridian and the factions' external networks. It is a vast ocean of unallocated offshore funds, mothballed server farms, unassigned mercenary platoons, stockpiles of rare earth metals, and political favors sitting in escrow. It is everything that could be used, but remains inert.

**The Backlog**

This is a faction's literal project backlog. It is the queue of theoretical operations, drafted blackmail schemes, and mapped supply runs sitting on the desks of their intelligence officers, waiting for the executive order to execute.

**Dispatch Tokens**

These are the executive authorizations—the green lights. Applying a Dispatch Token to an operation from The Backlog means the project has been fully funded, staffed, and pushed into active production for this specific Quarter.

**Accord Documents (Active)**

Biometric smart-paper contracts. When placed face-up on the scanner beds of the table, MIRROR reads the biometric signatures of the Factions involved and registers the treaty into ARBITER's canonical logic.

**Situation Reports (World Event Cards)**

These are not just intelligence briefs; they are global shockwaves crashing into the isolated shores of New Meridian. They are live feeds of stock market crashes in the Euro-Bloc, sudden atmospheric anomalies over the Pacific, intercepted diplomatic cables, and mass refugee migrations. They remind the room that the rest of the world continues to churn while they debate the future.

## 4. Tracks & Measurement Components

**The Session Timeline (Counting to 8)**

This is not a human clock, and it is not counting down. ARBITER is counting up to a specific, mathematically inevitable threshold of "Integration." It is measuring humanity's collective synthesis of the Chorus data. Quarter 8 represents the point of irreducible mass—the exact moment ARBITER will lock in humanity's response and open the return channel. It is the closing of a window. When that timeline hits 8, whatever fractured consensus, thesis, or question sits on the table will be irrevocably transmitted into the dark. The factions don't know exactly what this transmission will result in; they only know they each desperately need to be the one holding the pen when the time is up.

**The Initiative Strip & Faction Order Markers**

While this governs speaking order mechanically, it physically represents a faction's Operational Readiness and executive leadership effectiveness in that exact moment. ARBITER continuously ranks the factions, absorbing millions of micro-datapoints (supply chain efficiency, operative heart rates, budget allocation speeds). Crucially, ARBITER does not give a reason for its rankings. A faction might feel they are executing flawlessly, only for ARBITER to coldly drop them to last place—a terrifying reminder of its incomprehensible, opaque logic.

**Chorus Activity Track (The "Seismograph")**

This track was not requested by The Table, nor was it designed by human data-architects. ARBITER simply projected it onto MIRROR's interface during the early days of the Chorus Node, offering no label, no unit of measurement, and no explanation. "Seismograph" is just the frightened, colloquial metaphor the room settled on to describe the fluctuating graphic. Because no one actually knows what it means, it has become a canvas for factional hubris. The Directorate insists it's merely a measure of signal degradation; Ghost is certain it tracks the transmission's recursive proximity to full comprehension; The Guild argues it's measuring the physical and acoustic stress the signal puts on New Meridian's infrastructure. Every faction confidently asserts that their specific lens is "right," wielding their theory as proof of their superior grasp of the situation. The only terrifying consensus in the room is that the track reacts—when the factions execute operations, hoard resources, or escalate tensions in New Meridian, the 'seismograph' spikes. ARBITER provides no reason for this correlation; it just leaves the raw, unexplained data on the table, letting the factions tear each other apart over the shadows it casts.

**Public Standing Track**

This is an aggregate sentiment engine, but its scope is massive. It does not just track New Meridian; it tracks global sentiment. It scrapes the worldwide social mesh, measuring exactly how the billions of people watching the New Meridian feeds view the factions. It is the weight of humanity's judgment bearing down on the room.
