## Systemic Findings

These findings are only visible in comparison — not derivable from any single faction audit.

### S1 — Zero-cross-ceiling §9.2 is systemic across three factions

DIR (04-n118), GUI (04-n119), and SYN (04-n123) all show the same pattern: ceiling-power effects priced at mono. All three are native-mono-economy factions. Network has a cross ceiling (CA.1: E×1+F×1) but Findings-gated — Ghost-dependent (04-n126). Ghost has no ceiling inversion.

The §9.2 cross-resource pass (04-n118/119/123/126) addresses all four and should run together as a coordinated pass rather than faction-by-faction.

### S2 — The unbuilt modifier deck is the largest §5a implementation gap across the set

Three factions' §5a descriptions depend on modifier deck content that barely exists at L1:
- DIR: legislative modifier deck (PA cost reduction, world event extension) + military modifier deck (enforcement assets) — both fully undesigned
- NET: modifier deck self-feed is described as the "true engine" ("distributed, reactive, increasingly loud as the game progresses") — only 2 cards exist (MOD.1 complete, schema pending 04-n4; MOD.2 stub)
- GHO: passive Intel generation via game events near Ghost presence — no governing rule or modifier card implements it (→ 04-n143)

This is a structural L1 scope gap, not a missed individual card. Three factions cannot fully deliver their §5a deck-feel until 09-06 lands. The sustained-pressure identity of NET and the late-game power curve of GHO are both modifier-deck-dependent.

## C. Sustained Pressure Comparison

**Directorate — sustained-pressure pole**

Three Permanent/Seasonal standing board conditions (PA.1 Regulatory Override, PA.3 Entry/Exit Controls, PA.6 Standing Injunction) force other factions to route around them. These are environmental constraints, not reactive responses — Directorate acts at Beat 2, shapes the board, then lets Beats 3–4 resolve within the constraints it set. No other faction has this Beat 2 architecture. Standing-condition PAs persist across months, compound with CA.6/CA.7, and require active counter-action to remove. The unbuilt legislative modifier deck (PA cost reduction, world event extension) would deepen this further.

**Network — theoretically growing, mechanically thin at L1**

The modifier deck self-feed is the intended sustained-pressure vector ("louder each Quarter"). At L1 with 2 modifier cards, this is architectural intent. Network currently applies sustained pressure only through PA.3 Live Coverage (Seasonal compliance obligation on a named faction) — one card carrying the faction's entire sustained-pressure load. The "increasingly loud" character is a design promise that requires 09-06 to deliver.

**Guild — constructive-not-restrictive**

Structures are permanent and compound, but Guild's "pressure" is accretive — it builds, it does not constrain. Other factions adapt around Guild presence rather than being forced to respond to it. PA.1 Civic Works Mandate is the most coercive card; it locks territory by publicly declaring Guild's construction intent. Guild is better described as sustained-position than sustained-pressure: it holds ground that others must contest, not conditions that others must dismantle.

**Syndicate — episodic at high force**

CA.3/CA.8/CA.9/PA.1 are powerful territorial plays but single-event. Accord manipulation (CA.10/CA.11) creates sustained effects through active Accord tripwires — SYN.MOD.1 The Fixer (stub) is the closest mechanism to standing sustained pressure. Capital reserves mean each play hits harder, but the faction applies force in concentrated bursts rather than maintaining persistent table constraints.

**Ghost — point-disruption pole**

Zero standing board conditions. All cards immediate or Permanent-but-self-executing (no environmental constraints left on the board after resolution). Deep Cover voids a specific submitted token; Intercept removes a FRG entry; Pattern Match copies a single op. Precise strikes on specific plays, not sustained environmental shaping. Ghost's doctrine ("understanding must precede action") is compatible with this: Ghost prevents the wrong answer from being spoken at the right moment, not from being contemplated.

**Spectrum:** `DIR [sustained] ← NET (L1 thin) ← GUI (constructive/accretive) ← SYN (episodic high-force) ← GHO [point-disruption]`

The spectrum is doctrinally coherent: the faction most committed to institutional control of outcomes is the sustained-pressure pole; the faction most committed to concealment and precision is the point-disruption pole.

## H. Post-Audit Modifier Deck Additions (S128)

**1. Network Cascade Engine (`NET.MOD.7`–`9`)**
To fully realize Network's §5a identity ("increasingly loud as the game progresses") and resolve the L1 modifier deck gap identified in §S2 and §C, three net-new `ModReactCard`s were designed and stubbed to complete Network's suite:
- `NET.MOD.7` (**Community Amplifiers**): Net hand-growth. Draws 2-3 modifier cards triggered sequentially by any `public_act.resolved` board state change.
- `NET.MOD.8` (**Frequency Splitter**): Chain enabler. Triggers sequentially on Network placing a ModReact card, replacing itself and dropping physical Baryo presence to link noise together.
- `NET.MOD.9` (**Bandwidth Override**): The massive hand-flooder. Triggers on `status_marker.contested.placed` to dump 4 cards into Network's hand when the board gets messy.
*Conclusion:* Network can now passively convert the table's public noise into massive modifier card advantage and physical Baryo expansion, fully delivering on their "Wide Established Presence" win path via reactive disruption rather than generic area-control.

**2. Global Modifier Suite Renaming Pass**
All generic modifier placeholder names (e.g., "Proximity Signal", "Broadcast Reach", "Labor Claim") were renamed globally to reflect tangible Assets, Equipment, or Tactical Plans anchored strictly in faction doctrine. For example, Ghost's generic signals became *Perimeter Sensors* and *Sleeper Analyst*; Syndicate's "Accord Leverage" became *The Fixer*. All documentation (including `04___Card_System.md` and this audit's historical references) has been swept and updated.

## I. Post-Audit Ghost Modifier Suite Completion (S128)

To address Ghost's modifier deck gaps (specifically the "Flipped Intel" sinks) and execute the point-disruption mechanics missing from the L1 set, four net-new `ModReactCard`s were added:
- `GHO.MOD.5` (**False Flag**): Reverses a positive `public_standing.shifted` state change.
- `GHO.MOD.6` (**Supply Chain Tap**): Triggers on `resource.drawn_from_reservoir` to mirror the opponent's draw.
- `GHO.MOD.7` (**Sleeper Cell**): Triggers on `dominant_marker.placed` to immediately swap an opponent's chip for a Ghost chip, stripping the marker.
- `GHO.MOD.8` (**Local Sympathizers**): Triggers on `established_marker.placed` to remove an opponent's chip, stripping the marker.
*Conclusion:* All four late-game disruptions cost 1 native resource matching the target faction, cleanly enforcing the `Flip` pipeline where Ghost must first steal the opponent's economy to fund their point-disruptions.

## J. Post-Audit Guild Modifier Suite Completion (S128)

To address Guild's "Defense doesn't scale" risk and complete their passive structural income gaps (04-n2), four net-new `ModReactCard`s were added:
- `GUI.MOD.5` (**Company Town**): Passive intelligence. Triggers on `presence_chip.placed` in Guild-structured districts to draw 1 Faction Modifier card.
- `GUI.MOD.6` (**Emergency Reconstruction**): Triggers on `structure_block.removed(faction=Guild)`. Guild pays heavily to instantly place a replacement structure in an adjacent district.
- `GUI.MOD.7` (**Worker Retaliation**): Triggers on `structure_block.removed(faction=Guild)`. Guild floods the destroyed district with 2 presence chips.
- `GUI.MOD.8` (**Site Clearance**): The demolition and cleanup contract. Triggers on ANY `structure_block.removed` to pay Guild 1 district-native resource.
*Conclusion:* Guild's doctrine remains "constructive, not restrictive." They do not block attacks; instead, they make the consequences of targeting their infrastructure overwhelmingly accretive for Guild and punishingly tedious for the attacker.

## K. Post-Audit Directorate Modifier Suite Completion (S128)

To resolve Directorate's "early-game Mandate starvation" and fully realize their environmental board-shaping doctrine, five net-new `ModReactCard`s were finalized to complement their Military Enforcement branch (`MOD.1-3`):
- `DIR.MOD.4` (**Administrative Overhead**): Triggers on `accord.placed` to yield 1 Mandate, making Directorate the bureaucratic beneficiary of all diplomacy.
- `DIR.MOD.5` (**Emergency Appropriation**): Triggers when Directorate places a Permanent PA on their FRG, instantly yielding 2 Mandate to subsidize the heavy cost of their standing condition cards.
- `DIR.MOD.6` (**State of Emergency**): Triggers on `world_event.revealed`. Directorate places this card on their FRG for the Quarter, imposing a `boost=-10` difficulty penalty on any opponent PA targeting a district where Directorate is Established.
- `DIR.MOD.7` (**Eminent Domain**): Triggers on an opponent placing a structure. Directorate claims jurisdictional oversight by immediately placing a presence chip there for free, accelerating their win condition ("Established in more districts").
- `DIR.MOD.8` (**Asset Seizure**): Triggers on a PA placed in an Established district. Directorate confiscates 1 resource token directly off the target's card on the FRG, forcing them to pay a replacement fee or suffer partial-payment failure.
*Conclusion:* The full suite gives Directorate two arms: Military enforcement that physically rejects opponent expansion, and Legislative enforcement that subsidizes Directorate's massive standing orders by taxing the table.

## L. Post-Audit Syndicate Modifier Suite Completion (S128)

To resolve Syndicate's L1 modifier deck gaps around Accord manipulation, market swings, and liquidity, five new `ModReactCard` stubs were finalized:
- `SYN.MOD.4` (**Insider Trading**): Triggers on `public_standing.shifted(positive)`. Yields 1 Capital.
- `SYN.MOD.5` (**Short Squeeze**): Triggers on `public_standing.shifted(negative)`. Yields 1 Capital. (Together with MOD.4, Syndicate profits from any major political volatility).
- `SYN.MOD.6` (**Bounty Contract**): Triggers on an opponent placing a PA. Syndicate plays this on their FRG as a standing condition with 2 Capital on it, buffing the target PA with `boost=+20`. If the PA succeeds, the submitting faction claims the Capital.
- `SYN.MOD.7` (**Renegotiation Fee**): Triggers on `accord.corrupted`. Syndicate earns 2 Capital from the procedural friction of an Accord being textually altered by the table.
- `SYN.MOD.8` (**Hostile Takeover**): Triggers on `structure_block.removed(faction=opponent)`. Syndicate spends 2 Capital to instantly place 1 Presence Chip and 1 Structure Block in the cleared district, opportunistically buying up collapsed infrastructure.
*(Note: `SYN.MOD.3` Offshore Slush Fund's trigger was corrected to `accord.removed`, leaving an open design question on whether completion and breach should be separated).*
*Conclusion:* Syndicate's completed deck perfectly mirrors their doctrine: they are parasitic, transactional, and hyper-liquid, generating massive wealth from other factions' successes, failures, and diplomatic paperwork without exposing their own positions.

## M. Comprehensive Faction Playstyle & Win Condition Analysis

With the L1 Modifier Decks fully stubbed (MOD.1 through MOD.n for all factions), we can analyze how the combined work resolves the earlier gaps, delivers on the doctrinal playstyles, and creates distinct arcs from the opening game to the endgame.

### 1. Ghost (Information Parity & Point-Disruption)
**What was solved:** The deck now provides passive intelligence generation (triggering off opponent proximity) and late-game point-disruptions (swapping markers) without requiring Ghost to engage in direct territorial combat.
**Win Condition Path:** Reaching max Intel capacity or resolving specific Covert Ops un-attributed. They win by achieving total information parity and executing massive, untraceable board swings.
*   **Opening Game (Observation):** Passive stealth. Ghost seeds presence quietly and uses *Perimeter Sensors (MOD.2)*, *Institutional Trace (MOD.3)*, and *Signal Bleed (MOD.4)* to stockpile Intel Tokens off early opponent expansion.
*   **Mid Game (Interference):** Selective sabotage. Using *False Flag (MOD.5)* and *Supply Chain Tap (MOD.6)*, Ghost siphons opponent economy and reverses their PR gains while staying out of the primary territorial wars.
*   **End Game (The Flip):** Point-disruption. Ghost burns their accumulated resources on *Sleeper Cell (MOD.7)* and *Local Sympathizers (MOD.8)* to instantly yank Dominance/Established markers from opponents and replace them with Ghost presence, stealing the win condition just before the game ends.
*   **Remaining Gaps for Ring Mods:** Ghost relies heavily on opponent triggers. They need ways to manipulate ARBITER attention or secure specific high-value targets (like the Core ring itself) without leaving physical traces. Ring mods could offer ways to hide operations, spoof locations, or bypass standard marker restrictions.

### 2. Network (Asymmetric Noise & Momentum Scaling)
**What was solved:** Implemented the chain-drawing engine and massive hand-flooding mechanics. Network now actively converts board noise into physical Baryo presence.
**Win Condition Path:** Wide Established Presence (occupying the most districts with sheer volume and unignorable broadcast reach).
*   **Opening Game (Seeding):** Establishing broadcast infrastructure with *Pirate Transmitter (MOD.1)* and piggybacking on opponent builds with *Infrastructure Signal (MOD.5)*.
*   **Mid Game (Agitation):** Creating noise and punishing standing. Network drops *Troll Farm (MOD.2)* and *Street-level Agitator (MOD.6)* to provoke conflicts, tank opponent PR, and turn districts into Contested zones.
*   **End Game (The Cascade):** Unblockable momentum. Network triggers *Community Amplifiers (MOD.7)* and *Bandwidth Override (MOD.9)* off the mid-game chaos to draw massive hands. They then burn those cards via *Frequency Splitter (MOD.8)* to flood the board with Baryo presence.
*   **Remaining Gaps for Ring Mods:** Network's hand-size limits and resource generation might bottleneck their cascade. Ring mods could offer "free cast" mechanics, temporary hand-limit overrides in contested districts, or ways to weaponize the discard pile.

### 3. Guild (Infrastructure Monopolies & Unassailable Presence)
**What was solved:** Solved the "Defense doesn't scale" problem. Replaced active defense (shields) with passive retaliation and economic skimming off demolition.
**Win Condition Path:** Dominance in Ring 1 / Highest overall Structure count.
*   **Opening Game (Foundation):** Early infrastructure placement. Leveraging *Night Shift Crew (MOD.1)* and *Union Representative (MOD.2)* to build safely while taxing early opponent labor.
*   **Mid Game (Monopoly):** Locking down the Core and Institutions. *Core Premium (MOD.4)* and *Institutional Contract (MOD.3)* turn opponent ambition into Guild profits, while *Company Town (MOD.5)* feeds them modifier cards.
*   **End Game (Unassailable Presence):** Opponents trying to break Guild's structural dominance trigger a horrific trap. Demolition triggers *Worker Retaliation (MOD.7)* to flood the district with presence, *Site Clearance (MOD.8)* to pay Guild for the rubble, and *Emergency Reconstruction (MOD.6)* to instantly rebuild nearby, making attacks mathematically ruinous.
*   **Remaining Gaps for Ring Mods:** Guild is heavily static. If they get boxed out of Ring 1 early, they lack mobility. Ring mods could provide "logistics" mechanics—moving presence or structures between adjacent districts, or granting adjacency bonuses.

### 4. Directorate (Institutional Lock-Down & Taxation)
**What was solved:** Fixed early-game Mandate starvation and fully realized their environmental board-shaping doctrine through standing conditions on the FRG.
**Win Condition Path:** Established in more districts (Total Institutional Control).
*   **Opening Game (Jurisdiction):** Taxation and oversight. Using *Administrative Overhead (MOD.4)* and *Eminent Domain (MOD.7)* to claim free presence and Mandate while opponents build their early engines.
*   **Mid Game (Lockdown):** Blocking expansion. *Riot Squad (MOD.1)* and *Capital Suppression (MOD.2)* block enemy momentum, while *Emergency Appropriation (MOD.5)* funds Directorate's expensive permanent PAs.
*   **End Game (Martial Law):** Freezing the board state. *State of Emergency (MOD.6)* globally lowers opponent PA success probabilities, and *Asset Seizure (MOD.8)* bankrupts anyone targeting Directorate territory. The board becomes mathematically impossible to alter.
*   **Remaining Gaps for Ring Mods:** Directorate struggles against asymmetric/covert threats (like Ghost or Syndicate). Ring mods could provide "Surveillance" or "Audit" mechanics that force hidden information into the public sphere or temporarily disable React cards.

### 5. Syndicate (Parasitic Liquidity & Opportunistic Expansion)
**What was solved:** Delivered on Accord manipulation, market swings, and parasitic liquidity. Syndicate now profits off the game's meta-systems (diplomacy and PR).
**Win Condition Path:** Economic victory / Capital monopoly.
*   **Opening Game (Hedging):** Ensuring Syndicate profits from every early-game alliance. *Shell Corporation (MOD.2)* and *The Fixer (MOD.1)* generate wealth and control over early Accords.
*   **Mid Game (Speculation):** Market manipulation and mercenary funding. *Insider Trading (MOD.4)* and *Short Squeeze (MOD.5)* harvest PR volatility, while *Bounty Contract (MOD.6)* allows Syndicate to secretly fund proxy wars between other factions.
*   **End Game (Vulture Capitalism):** When the mid-game proxy wars inevitably destroy infrastructure or break Accords, Syndicate harvests the breach premiums (*MOD.3, MOD.7*) and buys the cleared land via *Hostile Takeover (MOD.8)*.
*   **Remaining Gaps for Ring Mods:** Syndicate relies on opponents *doing* things. If the table stagnates or colludes peacefully, Syndicate starves. Ring mods could offer "Market Shock" mechanics that force action, mandate resource transfers, or heavily penalize inaction.

*   **Syndicate (+4 Cards):** Design 4 PA/Mod variations (Potential focus: Additional Accord penalty variants or alternate market shocks).
*   **Guild (+4 Cards):** Design 4 PA/Mod variations (Potential focus: Alternate structural retaliation or logistics variants).
