## Model

Five factions arranged on a pentagram. Each point = one faction.

- **Most aligned** (perimeter neighbors, left + right): shared design space, doctrine overlap, card affinity candidates
- **Least aligned** (star-line connections, 2 steps away in each direction): opposed doctrine, minimum card overlap, narrative tension

## Arrangement (clockwise)

**Ghost → Network → Guild → Syndicate → Directorate**

## Rationale

**Perimeter adjacencies (aligned pairs):**
- Ghost / Network — both operate outside official channels; information-adjacent
- Network / Guild — shared community and working-class territory
- Guild / Syndicate — both deal in physical assets and resource control
- Syndicate / Directorate — both operate through visible, legitimate channels
- Directorate / Ghost — both access information others cannot (institutional vs. covert)

**Star-line tensions (opposed pairs):**
- Ghost vs. Guild — anonymity/evasion vs. direct physical confrontation
- Ghost vs. Syndicate — invisibility vs. deal visibility; trust vs. transaction
- Network vs. Syndicate — communal solidarity vs. transactional extraction
- Network vs. Directorate — grassroots resistance vs. institutional authority
- Guild vs. Directorate — labor vs. enforcement

## Application — Faction-Specific Card Narrative

Each faction-specific card carries three narrative voices:
1. **Owning faction** — primary voice
2. **One aligned neighbor** — sympathetic or envious framing
3. **One opposing faction** — skeptical, hostile, or warning framing
