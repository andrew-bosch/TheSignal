Here is a detailed architectural and narrative overview of the card systems discussed for **The Signal**, covering modifiers, interrupts, and counter-mechanics.

## System Overview: The Card Taxonomy

The card system functions as the mechanical engine for faction doctrine, translating political will and covert operations into physical board states.

| Card Category | Mechanical Function | Narrative Equivalent |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Action Cards** | Defines the core operation (covert or public) | Faction doctrine in motion (e.g., sabotage, construction) |
| **Modifier (Shift)** | Alters the 2d10 probability roll | Jammed frequencies, logistical friction, localized bribes |
| **Modifier (Shield)** | Alters rules (e.g., ignores World Conditions) | Single-use frequency hoppers, VIP security clearances |
| **Interrupt (React)** | Instantly fires upon a public board change | Rapid-response security details, automated pirate broadcasts |
| **Tripwire (Condition)** | Preemptively blocks or intercepts future actions | Localized signal-dampening fields, paid-off border inspectors |

---

## 1. Modifier Cards: Assets and Execution

Modifier cards represent the specific assets, tools, and resistance factions use to manipulate the difficulty or rules of an operation.

* **Covert Physical Commitment:** When submitted covertly, the Action card, the Modifier card, and all required resource tokens must be physically sealed inside the Dispatch Case together.
* **Absolute Economic Risk:** If an operation fails, or is intercepted at resolution, all committed resources are permanently spent and returned to the supply.
* **Visual Syntax (The Splay):** To prevent the game from stalling during complex resolutions, all critical data (Portrait shifts, 2d10 modifiers, resource costs) must be printed along a single edge. The Arbiter simply splays the cards to instantly calculate the aggregate mathematical and doctrinal impact.
* **The Multiplier Effect:** Massive operations amplify the Chorus's evaluation; a base Portrait shift of +/- 1 is multiplied by the total physical count of cards in the Dispatch Case.

---

## 2. Interrupts: Reacting to the Truth

Interrupts operate under strict constraints to prevent suspended "stacks" and ensure the board remains the single source of truth.

* **Observable Triggers:** An interrupt cannot trigger off hidden information or the secret contents of a Dispatch Case. It must trigger off something any player at the table can openly observe.
* **Immediate Resolution:** The game strictly forbids lingering or suspended states. When a card alters the board, that physical change resolves instantly.
* **Reacting to the Result, Not the Order:** You cannot play an interrupt to stop another interrupt from being played. You must react to the new physical reality the first interrupt creates.

### Narrative Example: The Chained Reaction

The Syndicate initiates an action to place a golden presence token in a northern district, representing the staging of heavy equipment for a new foundry. The Directorate plays an interrupt to instantly remove that token, representing a security detail shutting down the unauthorized site. The Network, wanting to expose the Directorate, cannot interrupt the Directorate's *order*. Instead, they must wait for the physical token to be removed from the board; that physical change triggers their own interrupt, representing operatives broadcasting the Directorate's overreach to the Sprawl.

---

## 3. Counters and Tripwires: Preemptive Area Control

Because a faction cannot retroactively block a React card, they must proactively establish signal jamming or operational friction before the trigger condition occurs.

* **World Conditions:** A card played openly that establishes a temporary but absolute rule for the Quarter (e.g., preventing React cards from triggering in a specific district).
* **Decaying Tripwires:** A condition that stops "the next X" actions and then disappears, forcing opponents to intentionally burn low-value actions to clear the trap.
* **Physical Interception:** A tripwire targeting covert actions can trigger off the physical act of submitting a Dispatch Case, catching whatever is inside indiscriminately.
* **Targeted Interception:** A tripwire targeting a specific *type* of operation (e.g., Sabotage) fires instantly during the Resolution Phase the moment the Arbiter publicly declares the action type.
* **The Execution Package Shield:** A player can proactively defend against a known tripwire by packing an expensive shielding modifier inside their Dispatch Case to bypass the trap when it fires.

### Narrative Example: The Cost of Paranoia

The Directorate establishes a localized signal-dampening field as an open World Condition. The Network needs to execute a Sabotage operation in that district. Knowing the tripwire exists, the Network seals a Sabotage Action alongside an expensive "frequency hopper" Modifier into their Dispatch Case, fully funding both. During resolution, the Arbiter reveals the Sabotage. The Directorate's dampener fires instantly, but the Network's frequency hopper bypasses the interference. The Sabotage succeeds, but the Network was forced to spend massive capital to punch through the established resistance.
