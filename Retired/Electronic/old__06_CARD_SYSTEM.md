# THE SIGNAL — Card System

**Document:** 06  
**Status:** Complete (system design) — Individual cards pending (Document 15 item 3.1)

---

## Design Philosophy

The card system solves a fundamental problem: how do you create 366 unique game objects that accumulate a persistent history without printing mechanical information on their faces?

The answer is **generic vessels** — cards that are physically minimal and narratively blank until ARBITER assigns them a function. A card's mechanical effect lives in ARBITER's database, not on the card. The card is just a key.

This enables:
- The same physical card to serve different functions across sessions and Covenants
- Complete history tracking without physical stickers obscuring content
- Zero rules text on any card — only narrative description and a code
- Full NFC/QR readability regardless of what the card is "doing" this session

## Physical Card Design

### The Generic Vessel Principle

Every card in the deck has the same basic physical design:
- Dark background (near-black)
- Subtle faction-agnostic geometric border
- Small NFC chip embedded in card body (PN532 compatible)
- QR code printed on reverse
- A 6-character alphanumeric identifier (e.g., "SG-0047")
- **No** rules text
- **No** mechanical information
- **No** faction markings (except Legacy Cards — see below)

The narrative description, mechanical effect, and all functional data live in ARBITER's database, keyed to the card's NFC ID.

### What Players See

When a player taps a card to their terminal's NFC reader:

```
TERMINAL DISPLAY — CARD READ

[Card type icon]
[Narrative title]

[2-3 sentence narrative description]
[Never mechanical language]

[If relevant: Age / Duration / Condition]

[ACTION BUTTONS based on card state]
  → HOLD (keep in hand)
  → ACTIVATE (use the card)
  → TRADE (offer to another faction)
  → REVIEW HISTORY (for Legacy/Contact cards)
```

The mechanical effect (what actually happens in the game engine) is never shown to the player. It is processed by ARBITER invisibly.

### Card Backs

All cards have an identical back:
```
ARBITER SIGNAL SYSTEM
OPERATIONAL ASSET

◈

HANDLE WITH APPROPRIATE
DISCRETION.

ARBITER TRACKS ALL ASSETS.
ALL CUSTODY IS RECORDED.
ALL USE IS NOTED.
```

## Card Type Specifications

### Event Cards (75)

Event Cards drive the world. One is revealed at the start of every Upkeep phase. Their public face contains a narrative description of something happening in New Meridian. Their private mechanical effect — processed by ARBITER invisibly — creates game state changes.

**The design rule:** The narrative must accurately describe the mechanical effect, but so obliquely that players cannot deduce the mechanics from reading it. If a card reduces Intelligence generation in Data Exchange districts, the narrative might read:

> *"Three analysts from the Chorus Research Complex have requested an unusual leave of absence simultaneously. Their supervisor has noted the requests but not yet acted on them."*

This is true — something about information-gathering has been disrupted — but players cannot reverse-engineer the specific mechanical penalty from it.

**Event Card Pool Structure:**

```
WORLD CONDITION EVENTS (30 cards)
  Category:       5 per world track × 6 tracks
  Effect:         Modify one world condition ±1-2
  Narrative tone: Matches the track being modified
  
DISTRICT EVENTS (25 cards)
  Category:       1-2 per major district type
  Effect:         Modify generation rates, security
                  levels, or effectiveness in specific
                  district types for 1-2 rounds
  Narrative tone: Local news, incidents, changes

FACTION EVENTS (10 cards)
  Category:       2 per faction
  Effect:         Benefit or challenge a specific faction
                  (faction not named — identifiable to
                  those who know what faction does what)
  Narrative tone: Industry news, personnel changes,
                  regulatory actions

CHORUS EVENTS (10 cards)
  Category:       Increasing intensity through the deck
  Effect:         Chorus Activity +1, reveal partial
                  layer hint (not the full reveal —
                  that's triggered by round number)
  Narrative tone: Scientific observations, anomalous
                  readings, researcher reactions
```

**Event Deck Construction Per Session:**

The 75 Event Cards are not all used in every session. ARBITER constructs a session deck of 10 cards (one per round, 2 unused) weighted by:
- Current Covenant state (troubled sessions get harder events)
- World Condition tracks (low Stability draws more crisis events)
- ARBITER's Stage (higher stages include more Chorus events)
- Random seed (ensures no two sessions are identical)

The ordering is set before Round 1 begins but not revealed. Players cannot predict which event is coming.

**Contact Card Loyalty Distribution:**

ARBITER assigns starting loyalty when a Contact Card is drawn, based on recruitment approach:

```
Professional recruitment:
  Devoted:  5%    (rare — they believe in the cause)
  Loyal:    25%
  Reliable: 45%
  Wavering: 20%
  Critical:  5%

Cultivated recruitment:
  Devoted:  15%   (longer relationship = genuine connection)
  Loyal:    40%
  Reliable: 30%
  Wavering: 10%
  Critical:  5%

Coerced recruitment:
  Devoted:   0%
  Loyal:      5%
  Reliable:  20%
  Wavering:  40%
  Critical:  35%   (coercion creates resentment)
```

Loyalty drifts over rounds. An asset that's never directed drifts toward Wavering. An asset that's regularly used and not exposed maintains or improves loyalty. An asset that's exposed (even without being burned) suffers loyalty loss.

### Counter Cards (30)

Counter Cards are dealt at session start — not drafted, not earned. They represent each faction's defensive readiness going into a session.

**Full Distribution Per Session:**

```
ARCHITECT (4 per session):
  Physical Security Counter ×2
  Structure Defense Counter ×2

GHOST (5 per session):
  Disinformation Shield ×2
  Counter-Surveillance Counter ×2
  Pattern Interrupt ×1

SYNDICATE (4 per session):
  Bribe Counter ×2
  Financial Fortification ×2

SIGNAL (3 per session):
  Static Burst ×2
  Ghost Protocol ×1

WARDEN (6 per session):
  Security Sweep ×2
  Detention Counter ×2
  Jurisdictional Block ×1
  Warrant ×1
```

**Unplayed Counter Cards** at session end are filed in the Archive. In subsequent sessions, ARBITER notes that a faction held counter cards in reserve — the Chronicle may reference this.

Counter Card scarcity is intentional. The Warden has 6 because they are the enforcement faction. The Signal has 3 because they operate offensively, not defensively. This asymmetry creates real strategic value — factions with fewer counters must either trade for cover from allies or accept that some operations will succeed against them.

**Age and Decay:**

Intelligence Cards have an age measured in rounds. ARBITER tracks the round they were generated.

```
Age:     Value:   Notes:
0-2      100%     Fresh — full mechanical value
3-4       75%     Slightly stale — most value retained
5-6       40%     Stale — significant value lost
7+        10%     Very stale — nearly worthless
                  (May still have narrative value
                   but minimal mechanical effect)
```

When a player taps an Intelligence Card, their terminal shows the age:
- *"This intelligence is current."* (Age 0-2)
- *"This intelligence was gathered recently. Some details may have shifted."* (Age 3-4)
- *"This intelligence is aging. Treat with appropriate caution."* (Age 5-6)
- *"This intelligence is historical. Treat accordingly."* (Age 7+)

Trading stale intelligence without disclosing its age is a common deception. The receiving faction can check age before accepting — but they must know to ask.

### Legacy Cards (16)

Legacy Cards carry physical inscriptions that persist across sessions and Covenants. They are the game's physical memory.

**Ascension Cards (8):**

One per unlock tier (Awakening and Ascendant) × 4 operatives per faction is too many physical cards — Legacy Cards are shared across the operative pool.

Instead: 8 general Ascension Cards, one issued when any operative reaches any unlock tier. The specific unlock is noted in pen on the card. This creates a physical artifact of achievement.

**Ascension Card face:**
```
OPERATIVE DESIGNATION: _________________

CAPABILITY LEVEL: ☐ AWAKENING  ☐ ASCENDANT

ACHIEVED:   Session ___  Round ___

DEMONSTRATED:

_______________________________________
_______________________________________
_______________________________________
[Space for ARBITER's Chronicle note]

THIS CAPACITY IS NOW PART OF THE RECORD.
WHAT WAS UNLOCKED CANNOT BE UNDONE.
```

**Scar Cards (6):**

Issued when a legacy event creates a permanent impairment. The mechanical effect is noted in ARBITER's database — the card carries the narrative.

**Scar Card face:**
```
OPERATIVE DESIGNATION: _________________

SESSION ACQUIRED: ___  COVENANT: ___

WHAT HAPPENED:

_______________________________________
_______________________________________
_______________________________________

WHAT REMAINS:

_______________________________________
_______________________________________

THE RECORD IS PERMANENT.
WHAT WAS CANNOT BE UNWRITTEN.
```

**Covenant Cards (2):**

One issued at the start of a Covenant, one at its end.

*Opening Covenant Card:* Records the starting state — world conditions, operative roster, which factions are human-controlled.

*Closing Covenant Card:* Records the end state — how the Covenant ended, the Portrait final assessment, the Accounting summary. Signed by ARBITER with a printed Chronicle fragment.

## NFC System

### Card Initialization

New cards begin as blank NFC chips. Before first use, ARBITER's administrative interface initializes each card:
1. Insert admin key to ARBITER unit
2. Admin mode activates
3. Present card to ARBITER's administrative NFC reader
4. ARBITER writes a permanent card ID to the NFC chip
5. ARBITER registers the card in its database
6. Card is now recognized by all terminals

Initialized cards cannot be re-initialized. The NFC ID is permanent.

### Session Assignment

At session start, ARBITER assigns each card its function for this session based on:
- Card type (Event, Contact, Equipment, etc.)
- Session context (which Covenant, which Session number)
- World state
- ARBITER's narrative judgment

A Contact Card that served as "Financial Analyst for Syndicate" in Session 3 might be reassigned as "Research Librarian for Ghost" in Session 5. The physical card is the same. The function is different. The history of both functions is preserved.

### QR Code Fallback

Every card has a QR code on its reverse. If NFC fails:
1. Player opens QR scanner mode on terminal
2. Scans card reverse
3. Terminal reads card ID from QR code
4. Same card data as NFC tap

QR codes are unique to each card and non-guessable (cryptographic hash component).

## The Burned Pile

Cards removed from play during a session go to the **Burned Pile** — not destroyed, just inactive.

What causes burning:
- Asset Card: Asset burned by exposure or detection
- Equipment Card: Single-use equipment consumed
- Intelligence Card: Information revealed (exposed) or aged out
- Operation Card: Operation card used (most are single-use)

**The Burned Pile is never shuffled back in during a session.** Burned is burned. Players can see how many cards are in the Burned Pile (public count) but not which cards (faction information).

At session end, ARBITER records which cards were burned, by which faction, in which round. The Chronicle notes significant burns.

**The Seized Pile:** Cards taken from another faction's hand go to the Seized Pile — still active, now in different hands. Physical card moves. History stays. The receiving faction can tap the card to see its current assignment (if any). ARBITER notes the custody transfer.

## Mark Stickers

Legacy Cards accumulate physical marks across sessions:

```
GOLD DOT:   Apex ability used (in good standing)
RED DOT:    Scar acquired
BLUE DOT:   Founding Figure designation
BLACK DOT:  Defection, disappearance, or the Turn
```

Mark stickers are applied at session end during the Chronicle reading. ARBITER announces each mark. The player peels a sticker from the provided sheet and places it on the card.

After several sessions, Operator Cards begin to read their history visually. A card with three red dots and one blue dot tells a story before anyone taps it to a terminal.

**Why physical stickers:** Digital tracking alone loses the tactile history. A player looking at their operative's card — holding the physical evidence of what their character has been through — creates a connection that a screen cannot replicate. The card has weight.

## Card Distribution and Setup

**At Session Start:**

1. ARBITER assigns Event Cards to session deck (10 cards selected from 75)
2. Contact Cards distributed to district piles (each type goes near its district zone on the mat — 4-6 cards per district type)
3. Equipment Cards shuffled into single deck, placed at table center
4. Operation Cards dealt: 2 per faction from generic deck + faction-specific cards distributed
5. Counter Cards dealt automatically: per faction allotment as above
6. Intelligence Cards held in reserve (generated during play)
7. Legacy Cards held by their operative's player from prior sessions

**During Play:**

- Contact Cards: Drawn face-down when Recruit action succeeds (player taps to terminal to learn who they recruited)
- Equipment Cards: Drawn when Equipment Purchase action taken
- Operation Cards: In hand from deal — can activate during Private Actions
- Counter Cards: In hand from deal — can only activate during Counter Window
- Intelligence Cards: Generated as named objects — specific to the operation that created them
- Event Cards: One flipped by ARBITER at Upkeep
