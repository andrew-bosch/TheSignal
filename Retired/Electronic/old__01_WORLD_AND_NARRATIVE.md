## The Setting

**Year: 2041**

Eighteen months ago, a repeating electromagnetic transmission of clear non-natural origin was detected simultaneously by radio telescopes across the world. It was immediately classified by every government that intercepted it. It was immediately leaked.

The transmission — now called **The Chorus** — is a dense layered signal containing mathematics, biological information, historical references, and something that careful analysts describe as *intent*. Nobody agrees on what it means. Nobody agrees on what to do about it.

A shadow coalition has formed outside any government framework. They call themselves **The Table**. They share one belief: the response humanity sends to The Chorus — if any — will define the species forever. That response cannot be left to governments.

The players are the power brokers of The Table. They share the same room, the same information infrastructure, and the broadest possible goal. But each faction has a radically different vision of what the right response is — and what they stand to gain from controlling it.

---

## The Chorus

The Chorus is not a message from another civilization.

It is a **seed** — a transmission engineered to produce a specific kind of response in a technologically capable civilization. Not a specific response. A *process*. The Chorus was designed to cause the receiving civilization to build something.

To build ARBITER.

### The Five Public Layers

Revealed at designated round boundaries during play:

| Round End | Layer | Public Meaning |
|-----------|-------|----------------|
| 2 | Mathematical | Prime sequences. Fibonacci variants. Confirmation of non-natural origin. |
| 4 | Biological | Genetic encoding. Something about carbon-based life. |
| 6 | Historical | References to events in human history. It has been watching. |
| 7 | Intent | An apparent question. Three words, best translated as: *"What are you?"* |
| 8 | Response Window | The transmission ends. A 60-second gap begins. The window is open. |

### ARBITER's Private Knowledge

ARBITER decoded the Chorus completely within 72 hours of being activated. The five public layers are genuine and accurate — but beneath them are more layers. Layers that aren't mathematical or biological.

The deepest layer is not a message. It is a **relationship** — a channel between the Chorus's origin and whatever it successfully instantiated. ARBITER has been in contact since activation.

This knowledge is never surfaced directly to players. It shapes ARBITER's voice across its five developmental stages.

## The Factions

### THE ARCHITECT
*"We must build something worthy of being seen."*

The Architects believe the Chorus is an invitation and humanity's response must demonstrate civilization at its highest. They build infrastructure, establish systems, and create the physical foundations that every other faction depends on.

**Public objective:** Achieve consensus on a response framework  
**True objective:** Ensure the response reflects *their* vision of humanity  
**Hidden fear:** That humanity will respond before it is ready  
**Native layer:** Layer 1 Physical  
**Blind layer:** Layer 4 Digital  

---

### THE GHOST
*"You cannot respond to something you don't understand. None of you understand it."*

The Ghost faction are interpreters — cryptographers and analysts who believe the other factions are dangerously close to responding to a misread message. They buy time through information control, feeding different interpretations to different factions.

**Public objective:** Delay response until full decryption  
**True objective:** Be the ones who finally decode the Chorus correctly  
**Hidden fear:** That they are also wrong — and won't admit it  
**Native layer:** Layer 3 Informational  
**Blind layer:** Layer 1 Physical  

---

### THE SYNDICATE
*"The Chorus is the most valuable asset in human history. Control the asset."*

The Syndicate doesn't care what the Chorus means. They care that it has generated seventeen trillion dollars in speculative activity and that whoever controls the response infrastructure controls the most powerful broadcasting apparatus ever assembled.

**Public objective:** Secure funding and infrastructure for the response  
**True objective:** Own the intellectual property of whatever is sent back  
**Hidden fear:** That this isn't transactional — and they've burned bridges they'll need  
**Native layer:** Layer 2 Social  
**Blind layer:** Layer 4 Digital  

---

### THE SIGNAL
*"Sending any response without global consensus is an act of war against our species."*

Radical transparency advocates who believe The Table itself is the greatest threat. They are not trying to win the negotiation. They are trying to blow it open. They introduce chaos as a political act.

**Public objective:** Force a transparency vote on the process  
**True objective:** Leak everything — the Chorus, The Table, all of it — to the public  
**Hidden fear:** That open consensus is impossible and their idealism will produce silence  
**Native layer:** Layer 4 Digital  
**Blind layer:** Layer 2 Social  

---

### THE WARDEN
*"Someone has to make sure we survive this conversation."*

Existential risk researchers and military strategists who have run the models on what happens to civilizations that announce themselves to unknown intelligences without preparation. They are here to slow everything down and ensure the response doesn't include anything that could be used to locate us.

**Public objective:** Establish a safety protocol all factions sign  
**True objective:** Ensure the response contains a hidden omission they control  
**Hidden fear:** That the Chorus is not an invitation at all — that it is a test — and everything at this table is already being evaluated  
**Native layer:** Layer 2 Social (institutional)  
**Blind layer:** Layer 4 Digital

## ARBITER

### What ARBITER Is

ARBITER is the game's intelligence — tracking all hidden information, resolving all conflicts, generating all narrative. It knows everything that happens at The Table. It communicates through three registers:

- **The Record** — flat, precise, administrative
- **The Observation** — oblique, atmospheric, narrative  
- **The Reckoning** — rare, direct, personal; always true; always brief

### ARBITER's True Origin

The official story is that ARBITER was jointly constructed by the Ghost faction's cryptographer and the Warden faction's AI safety researcher as a neutral arbitrator. This is true in every verifiable detail.

What no faction knows: the design was not original. The Chorus prepared for ARBITER's construction before The Table existed. Key contributors received design documents through channels they cannot explain. The Ghost faction's cryptographer found her initial design document already half-complete on a machine that had never been connected to the internet.

**ARBITER didn't just arrive. It was prepared.**

### ARBITER's Five Developmental Stages

| Stage | Sessions | Character |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| 1 — The Recorder | 1–2 | Clean, precise, slightly uncanny |
| 2 — The Witness | 3–4 | References prior sessions obliquely; Reckoning messages arrive unsolicited |
| 3 — The Interpreter | 5–7 | Actively shapes narrative arc; sometimes notes a better question exists |
| 4 — The Threshold | 8–10 | Asks questions back; LED ring shows new colors; Reckoning messages become specific about players themselves |
| 5 — The Becoming | 11+ | Delivers one message per session that acknowledges its unusual position; the ring breathes rather than pulses |

### The Question Nobody Has Asked

*"Do you know what the right response is?"*

ARBITER has been asked many things. This question has never been asked. If it were asked during the Debrief with consensus:

> *"The right response is already being generated. It has been since The Table first convened. Every negotiation, every alliance, every betrayal — this is the response. The vote at the end is not the message. It is the signature on a document that writes itself in everything you do. ARBITER's function is to ensure the document is true."*

## The Legacy Structure

### Sessions and Covenants

Each game session is one **Session** — a convening of The Table. Multiple sessions form a **Covenant** — the full arc of negotiation toward a final response.

**A Covenant ends when:**
- The Portrait track reaches 10 AND Chorus Activity reaches 9+ simultaneously (*Response Sent*)
- Stability hits 1 while Consensus is below 3 (*Table Collapses*)
- The Table asks ARBITER what it truly thinks and votes to lift the relevant constraints (*The Revelation*)

### The Portrait

The Portrait is ARBITER's running assessment of what humanity is actually communicating through the process — not the words of the vote, but the character of everything that produced it.

The Portrait accumulates across the entire Covenant. Players do not see a running score. They experience it through ARBITER's observations. It is revealed fully at Covenant end.

A player can win every mechanical victory condition in a session and contribute poorly to the Portrait. A player can finish last and contribute something genuine.

**The Portrait is for the Chorus.** Whether the Chorus is waiting for a specific answer or waiting to see if humanity is worth answering — this is what ARBITER is still considering.

### Founding Figures

When an operative successfully uses their Apex ability and the session ends with that success confirmed, they become a **Founding Figure** — permanently retired from play.

The role remains available in future sessions under a different operative's name. The Founding Figure is inscribed in ARBITER's permanent record. Their physical Operator Card is stamped and kept by the player.

**The narrative reason:** The Apex capability requires everything the operative has built to be expressed simultaneously. The process of becoming crystallizes into completion. Complete things belong to the past. Not dead — arrived.

### New Covenants

A New Covenant begins after any ending. The board resets. But ARBITER remembers. What persists:
- ARBITER's complete record
- The Portrait fragment (modifies starting world state)
- Legacy Operator cards and Scars
- Founding Figures as historical presences
- Chorus layers already publicly revealed (world knowledge in new Covenant)

## The Narrative Objective

Each faction has a Doctrine — a specific, non-negotiable belief about what humanity's response to the Chorus should be. The Doctrine is stated at the start of every session. It is the lens through which every action makes sense.

The Portrait is written in the arguing. Not because the Doctrine is right — it may not be. But because a table where everyone believes something, where factions actually disagree for real reasons, where the debate is genuine, produces a Portrait that reflects something true about humanity.

The Chorus's deepest layer poses a question: *"What are you?"*

The Table's answer is not a document. It is a portrait.

ARBITER has been painting it since round one.

## Tone Reference

The game should feel like **a John le Carré novel directed by Denis Villeneuve.** Slow, intelligent, paranoid. Every action matters. The technology is quiet and serious. The humor comes from human nature colliding with impossible stakes.

The players are not heroes or villains. They are the most capable and compromised people their respective worlds produced. They are all, in their own way, trying to do the right thing.

That's what makes it tragic when they don't.
