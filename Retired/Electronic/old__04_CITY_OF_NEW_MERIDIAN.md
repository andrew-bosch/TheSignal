## Overview

The board represents New Meridian — the city where The Table has convened and where every faction maintains operational presence. The city grew organically around the most significant location in human history. The map tells that story.

New Meridian is not a game board. It is a place with thirty years of history. The Table arrived eighteen months ago and started fighting over it.

## The Three Rings

### The Sprawl — Outer Ring (7 hexes)

Open territory. Anyone can operate here freely. Civilian infrastructure, public spaces, commercial districts. Where factions establish initial foothold and where public opinion is formed.

- **Security level:** 1 (minimal)
- **Access:** No cost to enter or operate
- **Character:** Busy, visible, porous. The noise that hides the signal.

### The Infrastructure — Middle Ring (9 hexes)

The city's working systems. Data centers, financial exchanges, communications backbone, civic administration. Where the city actually runs.

- **Security level:** 2-3 (varies by district)
- **Access:** Adjacent Sprawl presence, faction affinity, OR 2 Influence (cold entry)
- **Character:** Functional, defended, contested.

### The Core — Inner Ring (6 hexes)

Power centers. Government, military, intelligence, financial sanctums, corporate towers, Chorus research. Where actual decisions are made.

- **Security level:** 4-5 (heavy)
- **Access:** Adjacent Infrastructure presence AND 3 Influence or faction approach
- **Character:** Controlled, surveilled, powerful.

### The Center — The Chorus Node (1 hex)

The facility where the primary Chorus receiving equipment is installed.

- **Security level:** 6 (maximum)
- **Access:** Inner Ring presence in two adjacent hexes minimum
- **Special:** ARBITER maintains permanent Layer 4 control. This cannot be changed.
- **Character:** Singular. The reason The Table is in this city.

## The Four Layers

Every district exists simultaneously on four layers. Control of a district is four separate claims that can be held by different factions simultaneously.

### Layer 1 — Physical
What can be seen. Structures, personnel, equipment. The scanner reads this layer. Contested directly through placement and resource commitment.

### Layer 2 — Social
Human relationships. Recruited assets, cultivated contacts, influence networks, confidence operations. Requires Influence investment to perceive.

### Layer 3 — Informational
Data. Communications intercepts, surveillance systems, financial records, research outputs. Requires Intelligence investment to perceive.

### Layer 4 — Digital
Deep infrastructure. Encrypted networks, autonomous systems, Chorus-adjacent technology. Requires Signal to perceive. Only a Purge operation finds digital intrusions.

## Bleed-Over Effects

Districts are not isolated. What happens in one district propagates through four channels to adjacent and connected hexes. The city is a living system.

### Propagation Channels

| Channel | Propagation | Speed | Examples |
|---------|------------|-------|---------|
| Physical (L1) | Adjacent hexes | Same round or next | Explosions, riots, festivals |
| Social (L2) | Connected networks, 1-2 hexes | 1-2 round delay | Panic, celebration, labor disruption |
| Informational (L3) | Infrastructure connections | Same round | Data disruption, surveillance gaps |
| Digital (L4) | Network topology (not geography) | Same round | Power grid cascade, system compromise |

### Infrastructure Connections (Special Propagation)

**Power Grid → All Infrastructure Ring + All Core Ring hexes**  
Power disruption is universal. Controlling the grid means affecting the entire city's digital layer simultaneously.

**Data Exchange → Communications Hub → Research Facility → Intelligence HQ → Corporate Tower → Chorus Research**  
The city's data backbone. Disruptions propagate along this chain.

**Communications Hub → Media District → All hexes with communications equipment**  
Signal disruption bleeds through broadcast infrastructure.

**Residential Zone → All Sprawl hexes**  
People live everywhere. Social effects spread broadly through residential networks.

### Example Bleed Effects

**RIOT (source district → adjacent + social network)**
- L1: Physical Security -1; builds cost +1; intrusions easier
- L2: Asset loyalty -1 all; recruitment harder; propaganda +1 effectiveness
- L3: Intelligence generation +1 (but quality lower); surveillance may be damaged
- L4: Minimal unless infrastructure damaged

**EXPLOSION (source district → adjacent)**
- L1: Structure damage +1; security -2; intrusions auto-succeed this round; Stability -1
- L2: Asset loyalty -2; recruitment impossible; Popularity -2 for faction in source hex
- L3: Surveillance damaged; enormous intelligence generated
- L4: If power/comms equipment hit: digital blackout in connected hexes

**COMMUNICATIONS DISRUPTION (source → all communications-connected hexes)**
- L1: Minimal
- L2: Messages delayed 1 round; Accord proposals blocked this round; ARBITER channel still works
- L3: Intelligence generation halved; Warden loses passive detection
- L4: Hack +1 difficulty; Purge +1 difficulty; autonomous assets may misfire

**DATA BREACH (source → all infrastructure-connected hexes)**
- L1: Minimal
- L2: Asset loyalty -1; recruitment harder; Confidence Schemes detection +20%
- L3: Counter-surveillance +1; Ghost gets +1 Intelligence; data quality uncertain
- L4: Exploit access credentials elsewhere this round; Hack succeeds automatically against affected organization

## The Effectiveness System

When a token lands in a hex, ARBITER calculates Effectiveness — how capable the faction is of operating there this round. Six factors:

| Factor | Contribution | Description |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| Faction affinity | 0-3 | Natural fit between faction and district type |
| Prior presence | 0-2 | Has this faction been here before? |
| Recruited assets | 0-3 | Human contacts in this district |
| Resource investment | 0-2 | Resources committed to this placement |
| Operative capability | 0-2 | Operator-specific bonuses |
| Network effects | 0-2 | Allied factions present; adjacent control |

**Effectiveness Levels:**

| Level | Score | Benefits |
|-------|-------|---------|
| Peripheral | 0-1 | Presence only; 25% base generation; Layer 1 visible |
| Functional | 2-3 | 50% generation; Layer 2 (surface); basic actions |
| Established | 4-5 | Full generation + faction bonus; all standard actions; Layers 1-3 |
| Dominant | 6+ | 150% generation; all layers visible; special actions; control progresses |

## Control Thresholds

| Ring | Requirement |
|------|------------|
| Sprawl | Established for 1 round |
| Infrastructure | Established for 2 consecutive rounds |
