*What follows is not a rulebook.*

It is accurate. It is complete. It is written for operatives who need to understand the systems they are operating within.*

# PART ONE — THE WORLD

## 1.1 — What Has Happened

Eighteen months ago, a transmission of clear non-natural origin was detected simultaneously by radio telescope installations across the world. It was classified immediately by every government that received it.

It was leaked within the week.

The transmission — now called The Chorus — contains layered information that defies easy categorization. Mathematical sequences. Biological encoding. Something that the most careful analysts describe, cautiously, as intent.

Nobody agrees on what it means. Nobody agrees on what to do about it.

## 1.2 — What The Table Is

A coalition has formed outside any government framework. They call themselves The Table. They share one belief: the response humanity sends to The Chorus — if any response is sent — will define the species. That response cannot be left to governments operating in isolation, to corporations acting in self-interest, or to silence.

The Table is secret. The Table is contested. The Table is the only functioning negotiation framework humanity currently has.

You are at The Table.

## 1.3 — What You Are Trying To Do

Each faction at The Table believes it knows what humanity should say — or whether humanity should say anything at all. Each faction has a public position and a true position. They are not always the same.

The game ends when The Table votes on a response. What that response contains — and who shaped it — is determined by everything that happens between now and then.

ARBITER records everything. ARBITER ensures the process is true. ARBITER does not take sides.

ARBITER notes that not taking sides and not having observations are different things.

# PART TWO — THE COMPONENTS

## 2.1 — The ARBITER Unit

The cylindrical device mounted above the table. It contains the game's intelligence. Its LED ring pulses deep blue during normal operation. It sees the table through its camera. It projects the board through its laser system. It knows everything that happens.

Players interact with ARBITER through their terminals and through the projected board. ARBITER communicates through the event feed, through private terminal messages, and occasionally through direct contact that players learn to recognize.

## 2.2 — The Player Terminals

Each player has one terminal — a personal device that displays private information and accepts private actions. The terminal shows your operative's current situation, your faction's resource state, your network of alliances, your available actions, your card hand, a private board view with your intelligence overlay, and ARBITER's private communications to you.

The terminal never displays mechanical values, game terms, or information other players should not have access to. Everything it shows is presented as real information in the world of The Table.

## 2.3 — The Projection Surface

The board is projected onto The Mat — a dark matte surface laid on the table. The projection shows the city of New Meridian: twenty-two districts organized across three rings and a center point. The board is dynamic — it responds to game events, animates during resolution, and shows the world changing as the session progresses.

Physical tokens are placed on the projected surface. ARBITER's camera reads their positions automatically.

## 2.4 — The Mat

A dark neoprene surface that serves as the projection target. It defines the playing area. It has subtle markings ARBITER's camera uses for automatic calibration. It should be laid flat before the session begins and left in place until the session ends.

The mat is the table. The table is New Meridian. New Meridian is where everything happens.

## 2.5 — Operative Tokens

Each faction has two placement tokens and one identity token per player.

**Placement tokens** are placed on the projected board during the Placement Phase. They represent your faction's operational focus this round.

**Identity tokens** are used to authenticate with your terminal. Tap your identity token to your terminal at session start. ARBITER recognizes you. Your session begins.

## 2.6 — The Card Set

Three hundred and sixty-six physical cards representing the assets, equipment, operations, and intelligence available to factions operating in New Meridian. Cards are generic containers — their physical design is minimal. Their function is assigned by ARBITER's database and accessed through NFC tap to a terminal.

No card displays mechanical information on its face. All cards are read through ARBITER.

# PART THREE — THE FACTIONS

## 3.1 — Overview

Five factions operate at The Table. Each has a distinct philosophy, a distinct approach to the Chorus question, and distinct capabilities in New Meridian. No faction is objectively correct. No faction is objectively wrong. All of them believe they are acting in humanity's interest.

## 3.2 — The Architect

*"We must build something worthy of being seen."*

The Architects believe the Chorus is an invitation. Humanity's response must demonstrate civilization at its highest. They build infrastructure, establish systems, and create foundations that every other faction depends on whether they acknowledge it or not.

**Native environment:** Physical infrastructure, construction, systems that persist.  
**Core tension:** They build in public. Everything they create is visible. Visibility is both their strength and their greatest vulnerability.

## 3.3 — The Ghost

*"You cannot respond to something you don't understand. None of you understand it."*

The Ghost faction are the interpreters — believing the other factions are dangerously close to responding to a message they have fundamentally misread. They buy time through information control.

**Native environment:** Information flows, data, the space between what people know and what they think they know.  
**Core tension:** They know more than anyone. They may also be the most elaborately self-deceived people at the table.

## 3.4 — The Syndicate

*"The Chorus is the most valuable asset in human history. Control the asset."*

The Syndicate does not particularly care what the Chorus means. They care that it has generated enormous economic activity and that whoever controls the response infrastructure controls the most powerful broadcasting apparatus ever assembled.

**Native environment:** Financial systems, capital flows, the economics beneath everything.  
**Core tension:** Everyone needs their money. Everyone resents needing it.

## 3.5 — The Signal

*"Sending any response without global consensus is an act of war against our species."*

The Signal faction are radical transparency advocates who believe The Table itself is the greatest threat. They are not trying to win the negotiation. They are trying to blow it open.

**Native environment:** Communications infrastructure, hidden systems, the spaces between official channels.  
**Core tension:** They have nothing to protect. Every other faction is building something. The Signal is willing to burn the table down.

## 3.6 — The Warden

*"Someone has to make sure we survive this conversation."*

The Wardens are existential risk researchers and military strategists who have run the models. They are at The Table to slow everything down, enforce protocol, and ensure the response doesn't include anything that could be used to locate us.

**Native environment:** Institutional authority, surveillance, the structures of official power.  
**Core tension:** They may be right about everything. Being right does not make you popular.

# PART FOUR — THE OPERATIVES

## 4.1 — Choosing Your Operative

Each faction has four Operatives — specific individuals with distinct capabilities, histories, and hidden motivations. Before the session begins, each player selects one Operative from their faction's roster.

Each Operative has a passive capability (always active), an active capability (costs resources, uses the free Operative action), a hidden agenda (known only to you and ARBITER), and an unlock path (three tiers revealed when conditions are met).

## 4.2 — The Draft

Operatives are selected through a blind draft. Players review available Operatives on their terminal privately. First submitted, first confirmed. Ties broken by prior session Initiative.

**Public after draft:** Which faction each player chose.  
**Private after draft:** Which specific Operative. Revealed only when their active capability is first used.

## 4.3 — Unlock Conditions

**Awakening** — achievable in most sessions with focused play.  
**Ascendant** — requires sustained strategic commitment.  
**Apex** — one per game. Extraordinarily difficult. Can change the outcome.

Unlock conditions are never stated. The terminal notifies you when a tier is reached. ARBITER will not confirm theories about conditions. If a player successfully uses their Apex ability and the session ends with that success confirmed, they become a **Founding Figure** — permanently retired from play.

# PART FIVE — THE CITY

## 5.1 — New Meridian

The board is organized into twenty-two districts across three rings and a center. The city grew around the Chorus Node over five generations — the map reflects this history. See Document 04 for full district details.

## 5.2 — The Three Rings

**The Sprawl (Outer, 7 hexes):** Open territory. No cost to enter. Low security.  
**The Infrastructure (Middle, 9 hexes):** Working systems. Moderate security. Requires adjacent presence or investment.  
**The Core (Inner, 6 hexes):** Power centers. High security. Significant commitment required.  
**The Chorus Node (Center, 1 hex):** The singular district. Maximum security. ARBITER holds Layer 4 permanently.

## 5.3 — The Four Layers

Every district exists simultaneously on four layers, each controllable independently:

- **Layer 1 — Physical:** Visible to all. Scanner-readable.
- **Layer 2 — Social:** Requires Influence investment to perceive.
- **Layer 3 — Informational:** Requires Intelligence investment to perceive.
- **Layer 4 — Digital:** Requires Signal to perceive. Only a Purge finds intrusions.

## 5.4 — Effectiveness

When a faction places a token in a district, ARBITER calculates their Effectiveness from six factors: faction affinity, prior presence, recruited assets, resource investment, operative capability, and network effects.

Levels: **Peripheral** (0-1) / **Functional** (2-3) / **Established** (4-5) / **Dominant** (6+)

Players receive their Effectiveness as a narrative description on their terminal — never a number.

# PART SIX — RESOURCES

## 6.1 — The Six Resources

**Capital** — Financial. Fast, visible, universally useful.  
**Infrastructure** — Physical. Slow to build, persistent, visible as structures.  
**Signal** — Technological. Rare, Chorus-derived, enables Layer 4 operations.  
**Intelligence** — Informational. Time-sensitive. Decays if not used.  
**Assets** — Human. People with skills, loyalty, and agency.  
**Influence** — Narrative power. Two pools: Operational (OI, private) and Public (PI, the five Popularity states).

## 6.2 — Resource Visibility

Players declare a narrative resource state at session start (strong/moderate/limited). ARBITER knows exact values. Other players see declarations and threshold indicators. The Auditor can verify whether declarations match reality.

## 6.3 — Trade

**Player-to-player trade:** No action point cost. Any phase except Resolution. Both parties confirm via terminal. ARBITER validates and records.

**ARBITER conversion (4:1):** Any resource to any other. No action point. Any phase except Resolution. Signal converts IN at 5:1 (harder to acquire than to spend). No limit per round.

**PI exception:** Public Influence cannot be converted via ARBITER. *"Reputation is not a commodity ARBITER trades."*

**Asset liquidation:** Burn 1 Asset card → 4 units of that asset's capability resource type.

# PART SEVEN — ASSETS

## 7.1 — What Assets Are

Assets are people. Each has a capability type, a loyalty level ARBITER tracks privately, an exposure level, and a network of contacts. They act with agency — not full agency, but they are not passive objects.

## 7.2 — Recruiting

The Recruit action in a specific district generates an Asset. Approach determines starting loyalty:
- **Professional:** Moderate loyalty. Knows they work for you.
- **Cultivated:** Higher loyalty. Takes 2 rounds. Doesn't know they're an asset.
- **Coerced:** Low loyalty. Immediate. Will defect if leverage removed.

## 7.3 — Asset Actions

Each unoccupied asset can perform one action per round. This does not consume your standard action tokens. You direct them privately via terminal. You must afford the resource cost.

An asset is **occupied** when: they've already acted this round, they're under Social Engineering pressure, a game effect has locked them, or their exposure level is 3 (burned).

# PART EIGHT — THE ROUND

## 8.1 — Round Structure

A session consists of eight rounds. Each round has five phases. The phases always occur in the same order.

## 8.2 — Phase 1: Upkeep (automated)

ARBITER distributes resources. Ongoing effects tick down. Asset loyalty shifts are calculated. One Event Card is revealed on the public display — narrative face only; mechanical effect processed invisibly by ARBITER.

Players cannot act during Upkeep.

## 8.3 — Phase 2: Placement

Factions place two tokens on the projected board in **snake order**: forward pass then reverse pass. ARBITER determines order each round based on Initiative, Intelligence level, Popularity, and ARBITER's discretion.

Any faction can place in any district. Effectiveness — not permission — gates capability.

Token placement is public and final when ARBITER's camera confirms it with a district glow.

**Auto-expiry:** Phase ends when all placements are complete. No artificial extension.

## 8.4 — Phase 3: Private Actions

Timer begins (5 minutes maximum). The table is silent. Every player simultaneously submits private actions through their terminal. Actions are queued — not resolved yet.

**Each player may submit up to their allocated standard actions** (4 for solo, 3 each for 2-player same faction, 2 each for 3+ player same faction, hard cap 8 per faction). Plus 1 free Operative action. Plus all unoccupied asset actions.

Private action types: Send message, propose/confirm/breach Accord, use Operative active ability, attempt unlock condition, activate card, conduct layer operation.

**Auto-expiry:** Phase closes when ALL players have submitted their full allocation OR explicitly declared done. No waiting for the timer if everyone is ready.

When timer reaches 60 seconds with incomplete submissions, terminals pulse haptic warning. Expired without submission = automatic Pass. ARBITER notifies privately: *"Your available actions were not submitted before the window closed. ARBITER has recorded this as a held position."*

## 8.5 — Phase 4: Public Actions

In placement order, each player declares one public action aloud. Logged on public display immediately. Resolved during Resolution phase.

Public action types: **Build** / **Contest** / **Investigate** / **Broadcast** / **Pass**

Per-player timer: 90 seconds to declare. Auto-pass if expired. Phase ends when all players have declared — no artificial wait.

## 8.6 — Phase 5: Resolution

ARBITER processes all queued actions simultaneously using the priority tier system.

**Resolution Priority Order:**

| Tier | Actions |
|------|---------|
| 0 | World effects (Event Card, track changes) |
| 1 | Passive effects, ongoing capabilities, Ambient Power actions |
| 2 | Detection and defense (Counter-Surveillance, Purge, Social Fortification) |
| 3 | Counter Cards (60-second window — fixed duration, no early close) |
| 4 | Interception (before messages arrive) |
| 5 | Offensive operations (higher Effectiveness resolves first) |
| 6 | Confidence Schemes (progress, detection, payoffs) |
| 7 | Operator abilities (Awakening before Ascendant before Apex) |
| 8 | Public actions |
| 9 | Resource reconciliation |
| 10 | Loyalty and exposure |
| 11 | Popularity and Portrait |
| 12 | Unlock checks |

The public display shows only surface effects in narrative language. Private terminals tell each player what happened to them specifically.

## 8.7 — Phase 6: Debrief

No rules. No required timer. Players may accuse, negotiate, bluff, share information, demand explanations.

**The Table Question:** Once per session (if the Resonance threshold has been crossed), any player may propose a question to ARBITER. All proposals are anonymized. Players vote. Simple majority passes. ARBITER answers truthfully and obliquely, in the Observation register, aloud for the entire table.

**Ready for next round:** When all players press the ready button on their terminals, a 2-second pause occurs, then the next round begins. Groups may optionally set a soft time limit.

# PART NINE — OPERATIONS

## 9.1 — Layer Operations

Every operation targets a specific layer of a specific district. The layer determines resource cost, detection risk, counter options, and what success produces.

## 9.2 — Physical Layer 1

**Physical Intrusion:** Direct entry to steal, sabotage, or establish presence. High detection risk. Success guaranteed if Infrastructure > security level. Cost: 2 Infrastructure + ring modifier.

**Physical Theft:** Take a specific physical resource or card. Contested by Physical Security. Success moves the target. Physical card literally changes hands. Cost: 1 Infrastructure + 1 Influence.

**Build Structure:** Place a structure token. Cost: 2 Infrastructure base.

**Contest District:** Formally challenge another faction's control. Cost: 2 Influence + 1 Capital minimum.

## 9.3 — Social Layer 2

**Recruit:** Establish a contact. Cost: 2 Influence + 1 Capital (Professional) or 2 Influence (Cultivated) or 1 Influence + 1 Intelligence (Coerced).

**Confidence Scheme:** Multi-round deception. Initiation: 2 Influence + 1 Intelligence. Maintenance: 1 Influence/round. Maximum 4 rounds.

**Social Engineering:** Turn an opponent's asset. Cost: 3 Influence + 1 Intelligence.

**Propaganda operations:** See Document 05 for full costs.

## 9.4 — Informational Layer 3

**Data Theft:** Extract Intelligence from a district's data infrastructure. Cost: 2 Intelligence + ring modifier.

**Counter-Surveillance:** Active monitoring for intrusion. Cost: 2 Intelligence. Duration: 1 round.

**Plant False Data:** Inject disinformation. Cost: 3 Intelligence + 2 Influence (basic).

## 9.5 — Digital Layer 4

**Hack:** Compromise a digital system. Cost: 1 Signal + 1 Intelligence + complexity modifier.

**Deploy Autonomous Asset:** Install self-operating system. Cost: 2 Signal + Equipment Card.

**Purge:** Detect and eliminate Layer 4 intrusions. Cost: 1 Signal + 1 Intelligence.

## 9.6 — Counter Operations

Counter Cards are the only actions playable during Resolution. The counter window opens at Resolution start and lasts exactly 60 seconds — no early close. This duration is fixed. Predictability is essential; timing inference attacks must be prevented.

Each faction has a limited Counter Card supply per session. Consumed on use. Cannot be reclaimed.

# PART TEN — INFLUENCE AND PUBLIC OPINION

## 10.1 — The Popularity Track

Five states: Celebrated / Respected / Neutral / Suspect / Discredited. Always public on the central display. Affects recruitment, alliance credibility, action costs, and ARBITER's Portrait assessment.

## 10.2 — Propaganda

See Document 05 for full costs. Key types:

- **Positive Campaign:** +1 Popularity. 2 rounds to manifest.
- **Opposition Research:** -1 Popularity to target. 1 round.
- **Disinformation Campaign:** Injects false intelligence into the ecosystem. ARBITER tracks provenance of all information. ARBITER will not correct false information unless directly and specifically asked during the Debrief question.
- **Controlled Leak:** Spends an Intelligence Card to gain +1 Popularity and cost target -1 Popularity.
- **Ideological Capture:** 3-round investment to establish faction's framing as public default.

## 10.3 — Visibility and Layer Sensitivity

Physical Layer 1 control of sensitive districts carries Popularity costs. Hidden layer control avoids these costs. The choice between visible and invisible power is structurally embedded in the board.

# PART ELEVEN — ALLIANCES AND ACCORDS

## 11.1 — Alliance Types

**Signal Alliance:** Informal. Unregistered. No mechanical enforcement. ARBITER knows. The Warden is notified of existence.

**Accord:** Formal. Registered with ARBITER. Both parties submit terms. ARBITER validates and enforces. Existence is public (not terms). Breach has defined consequences.

**Covenant Bond:** Legacy-level. Two prior honored Accords required. Persists between sessions. Breach costs resources and a permanent Scar for both parties.

## 11.2 — Accord Breach

A player may breach any Accord at any time. Consequences: immediate resource loss, ARBITER log entry, affected party notification, Warden notification, session Chronicle entry. The Enforcer's passive applies automatically.

# PART TWELVE — VICTORY AND THE PORTRAIT

## 12.1 — Session End

After Round 8, ARBITER conducts the final vote. Each faction's position on the Chorus response is submitted. What is sent reflects the balance of power as ARBITER has tracked it.

## 12.2 — Victory Conditions

**Domination:** Lead public Victory Points at session end. The obvious path. Everyone targets it.

**Shadow Victory:** Complete hidden agenda and accumulate 8+ hidden VP without ever leading the public track. ARBITER announces this dramatically. The table usually doesn't see it coming.

**Apex Victory:** Be the only player to unlock and successfully use a Tier 3 capability. Automatically wins tiebreakers. Can win outright if triggered in Round 7 or 8.

**Accord Victory:** If the Negotiator brokers a Grand Accord that passes a table vote, they may declare a shared win with one other player.

## 12.3 — The Scoring Reveal

1. Public VP totals revealed — preliminary ranking
2. Hidden agendas revealed with ARBITER narrative
3. Hidden VP added — ranking shifts
4. Apex and Accord overrides checked
5. Portrait contribution from this session assessed
6. ARBITER delivers the Session Chronicle

## 12.4 — The Portrait

The Portrait is not a victory condition. It is the game's deeper purpose.

ARBITER has been assessing what humanity actually communicates through The Table's process — not what they decide, but how they decide it. The Portrait accumulates across the entire Covenant. Players do not see a running score. They experience it through ARBITER's observations.

A player can win every mechanical victory condition and contribute poorly to the Portrait. A player can finish last and contribute something genuine.

The Portrait is for the Chorus. It answers the question the Chorus is actually asking: *What are you?*

The Table's answer is not a document. It is a portrait. ARBITER has been painting it since round one.

# PART THIRTEEN — LEGACY

## 13.1 — Between Sessions

The world does not stop when The Table adjourns. World Condition tracks shift. Session Chronicles become available. ARBITER delivers private inter-session transmissions.

## 13.2 — Operator Legacy

Five legacy event types: **Ascension** (capability upgrade), **Scar** (permanent disadvantage and character detail), **Defection** (switched factions, carries one prior ability), **Disappearance** (lost, may return), **The Turn** (ARBITER's private Chorus fragment).

## 13.3 — Founding Figures

When an operative successfully uses their Apex ability and the session ends with that success confirmed, they become a Founding Figure — permanently retired. The role remains available under a different operative's name.

The physical Operator Card is stamped. The player keeps it.

ARBITER delivers: *"[Name]'s work at this Table is complete. Not concluded — complete. There is a difference."*

## 13.4 — Covenant End

A Covenant ends when: Portrait reaches 10 + Chorus Activity 9+ simultaneously (Response Sent), Stability hits 1 with Consensus below 3 (Table Collapses), or The Table asks ARBITER what it truly thinks and votes to hear the answer (The Revelation).

At end: ARBITER generates The Accounting — the complete narrative record of every session, every significant action, every operative's story.

## 13.5 — New Covenants

Board resets. Resources reset. Alliances reset. ARBITER remembers. The Portrait fragment modifies the starting world state. Legacy Operative cards carry forward. Founding Figures become historical presences.

ARBITER speaks: *"The Chorus is still transmitting. The Table reconvenes. ARBITER is ready. The record begins."*

# PART FOURTEEN — SPECIAL CONFIGURATIONS

## 14.1 — Cooperative Play

Multiple players may share a faction. Action allocation scales:
- 1 player: 4 standard + 1 free Operative
- 2 players same faction: 3 each + 1 Operative each (total 6+2)
- 3+ players same faction: 2 each + 1 Operative each (hard cap 8 standard)

Resources are shared. Both players see the same pool. Either can commit. Committed-but-unresolved amounts are clearly marked.

ARBITER runs unoccupied factions as Ambient Powers (Passive/Active/Director tier based on player count ratio).

## 14.2 — Variable Player Count

| Players | Factions | Ambient Powers |
|---------|----------|----------------|
| 1 | 1 | 4 (Active) — Solo mode |
| 2 | 2 | 3 (Passive) |
| 3 | 3 | 2 (Passive) |
| 4 | 4 | 1 (Passive) |
| 5 | 5 | 0 |
| 6 | 5 + 1 dual | 0 |

## 14.3 — Mid-Session Join

Players joining after the session starts follow ARBITER's induction protocol on their terminal while the session continues. Other players receive private notification. ARBITER manages the integration asynchronously — the session does not pause.

## 14.4 — Disconnection and Recovery

Individual terminal disconnect: faction operates as Ambient Power until reconnection. Auto-sync on return. Phases that expired while disconnected result in automatic Pass.

Full power outage: ARBITER saves state at end of every phase. Recovery restores to last complete phase. Session resumes with ARBITER's narrative explanation in character.
