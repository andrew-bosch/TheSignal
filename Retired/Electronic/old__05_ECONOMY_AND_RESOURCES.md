# THE SIGNAL — Economy & Resources

**Document:** 05  
**Status:** Complete (starting values — require playtest calibration)

---

## Design Philosophy

The economy must:
1. Create genuine resource scarcity requiring real tradeoffs
2. Reinforce faction identity through resource type alignment
3. Make the 4:1 ARBITER conversion a meaningful last resort, not routine
4. Show clear trajectory from tight early game to capable late game
5. Make assets feel like multiplicative investment with delayed returns

---

## The Six Resources

### CAPITAL
**Hard Power — Financial**

Fast to move, easy to generate, universally useful. Pays for operations, bribes, and physical infrastructure. Highly visible above threshold — the Auditor tracks it, the Launderer hides it.

- Generated by: Financial district control, Syndicate passive, Banker interest
- Decays: Never (but visible above 20 units — Auditor passive activates)
- Stolen: Yes — Physical Theft on Financial hexes
- Visibility threshold: Publicly noted above 20 units

---

### INFRASTRUCTURE
**Hard Power — Physical**

Installed capability. Slow to build, persistent, physically present on the board as structures. Generates other resources passively.

- Generated by: Building actions, Architect passive, Infrastructure hexes
- Decays: Only if damaged by operations or events
- Stolen: Partially — can be sabotaged, not directly taken
- Visibility: Visible as structures on the projected board

---

### SIGNAL
**Hard Power — Technological**

Chorus-derived capability. Rare, powerful, enables Layer 4 digital operations. Cannot be generated through conventional financial or physical means. Accumulates rather than cycling quickly.

- Generated by: Chorus Node control, Research/University hexes, Signal faction passive
- Decays: 10% per round above 10 units (soft cap)
- Stolen: Yes — Digital Layer operations
- Visibility: Almost never visible; revealed only through use

**Special conversion rate:** Signal costs 5:1 to convert TO (all other resources are 4:1). Signal OUT is standard 4:1.

---

### INTELLIGENCE
**Information / Hybrid**

Actionable data. Time-sensitive — fresh intelligence is powerful, stale intelligence becomes noise. Requires investment to acquire, immediately useful.

- Generated by: Data Node hexes, Ghost passive, surveillance assets
- Decays: See decay table below
- Stolen: Yes — Data Theft operations
- Visibility: Never directly visible to other players

**Intelligence Decay Table:**

| Amount Held | Decay Rate | Converts To | Conversion Rate |
|-------------|------------|-------------|-----------------|
| 1–6 units | No decay | — | — |
| 7–12 units | 25% | OI | 0.5:1 |
| 13+ units | 50% | OI | 0.25:1 |

This prevents Ghost from stockpiling and forces active intelligence use.

---

### ASSETS
**Soft Power — Human**

People with skills, access, and loyalty. Not an abstraction — each Asset is a specific person tracked by ARBITER. Asset count implies a faction's operational footprint and cannot exceed the hexes where they maintain presence.

- Generated by: Recruitment actions (not passive generation)
- Decays: Loyalty drifts when neglected; exposed assets become burned
- Stolen: Via Social Engineering (loyalty reduction)
- Visibility: Implied publicly when 3+ assets active ("significant local relationships")

---

### INFLUENCE — Two Pools
**Narrative Power — Dual Form**

Influence does two different jobs. These pools are explicitly separated.

**Operational Influence (OI)** — Private currency of power. Favors, leverage, relationships, access. Used to fund social actions. Never decays. Converts at 2:1 to PI when PI is Respected or higher (reputation opens doors).

**Public Influence (PI)** — Reputation and perceived legitimacy. Exists as five states: Celebrated, Respected, Neutral, Suspect, Discredited. All states are always public on the central display.

**Pool Conversion:**
- OI → PI: 3 OI → 1 PI unit (requires 1 action point; manufacturing reputation is expensive)
- PI → OI: 2 PI units → 1 OI (no action point; available only at Respected or above)
- PI cannot be ARBITER-converted — public reputation is not a commodity

**Asset action rules:**

- Asset must not be occupied (see occupied definition)
- Player must afford the resource cost

## Trade Rules

### Player-to-Player Trade
- **No action point cost** — trade at any time except Resolution phase
- Both parties confirm via terminal
- ARBITER validates resources exist and executes transfer
- ARBITER records all trades in the event log
- Public trades (Debrief phase) noted in event feed without amounts
- Private trades (Private Actions phase) not announced

**What can be traded:** Capital, Infrastructure, Signal, Intelligence (fresh or stale — age disclosed on request), OI, Cards (with conditions), Asset Points  
**What cannot be traded:** Public Influence PI, loyalty, unlock progress, Accord terms without re-registration

**Contact Card trade rule:** Receiving party does NOT see loyalty or exposure before transfer unless the sending party explicitly reveals it. Buying a wavering asset without disclosure is possible. ARBITER notes deceptions.

**Multi-party trades:** Three factions can trade simultaneously. ARBITER validates the full chain before executing any part — no party is left holding nothing.

### ARBITER Conversion (4:1)
- Any resource → any other resource
- Rate: 4 → 1
- **Exception:** Signal IN costs 5:1 (Signal is harder to acquire than to spend)
- No action point required
- Any phase except Resolution
- No limit per round
- Asset liquidation: 1 asset burned → 4 units of that asset's capability resource type

**Public notification on conversion:**
> *"Resource reallocation processed through Table infrastructure. A faction has converted at significant cost. The reason is their own."*

No attribution. The fact that someone needed something urgently becomes mild table tension.

**PI conversion exception:** ARBITER cannot convert Public Influence to other resources. If attempted:
> *"ARBITER cannot convert public reputation to operational resources through the Table infrastructure. Reputation is not a commodity ARBITER trades. What others think of you is not yours to sell."*

## Layer Economic Alignment by Faction

Each faction has a native layer (most efficient) and a hostile layer (least efficient).

| Faction | Native Layer | Efficient | Weak | Hostile |
|---------|-------------|-----------|------|---------|
| Architect | L1 Physical | L2 Social | L3 Info | L4 Digital |
| Ghost | L3 Informational | L4 Digital | L2 Social | L1 Physical |
| Syndicate | L2 Social | L1 Physical, L3 | — | L4 Digital |
| Signal | L4 Digital | L3 Info, L2 | — | L1 Physical |
| Warden | L2 Social (institutional) | L1, L3 | L4 | — |

**Effect of layer efficiency:**
- Native layer: +25–50% generation, action costs -1
- Efficient layer: Standard generation and costs
- Weak layer: Reduced generation, action costs +1
- Hostile layer: Minimal generation, action costs +1–2

## Ambient Power Economy

ARBITER-controlled factions generate simplified resources:

| Tier | Generation | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| Passive | 4 primary/round | Faction's primary resource only |
| Active | 6 primary + 2 secondary | Limited asset income (max 4 assets) |
| Director | 15 across all types | Full capability; ARBITER never wastes |

**Player bribery of Ambient Powers:** A player can deposit resources with ARBITER earmarked for an Ambient Power faction. ARBITER uses the bribe to improve that faction's position, causing them to favor the bribing faction in their action choices. This is the only player-Ambient Power interaction. It is not an alliance.

## Balance Notes (Pre-Playtest)

**Ghost adjustment needed:** Ghost's Intelligence generation is theoretically very high (15–20 units/round with full L3 control). The decay rule prevents hoarding but the generation rate may still feel overwhelming. Watch for Ghost having effectively unlimited intelligence operations.

**Syndicate Capital dominance:** Syndicate can reach 20+ Capital/round with financial district control. The affinity bonus cap (2 districts max) prevents exponential scaling. Monitor whether Capital surplus buys too much flexibility through ARBITER conversion.

**Warden OI surplus:** Warden can reach 11–15 OI/round. The primary weakness (no Signal) creates a genuine constraint. The 5:1 Signal conversion means 3 Signal costs Warden 15 OI — extremely painful but possible. Monitor whether this creates total digital blindness or acceptable strategic limitation.

**Signal resource poverty:** Signal has no Capital and no Infrastructure. Every build action and every recruitment requires either trading or the 4:1 conversion from their surplus OI/Signal. This should feel correct for a faction that philosophically opposes physical infrastructure. Watch for Signal being completely unable to act when their natural operations are countered.
